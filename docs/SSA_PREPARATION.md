## EXAM 1: PSYCHIATRIC EXAMINATION (Feb 20)

### Format
- **Type:** Telehealth (video/phone)
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes
- **Examiner:** SSA-contracted psychiatrist
- **Purpose:** Evaluate psychiatric/cognitive disability claims (AuDHD)

### What They're Evaluating (Paragraph B Criteria)

SSA uses these four functional areas:

1. **Understand, remember, or apply information**
   - Can you follow instructions?
   - Can you learn new tasks?
   - Can you remember important information?

2. **Interact with others**
   - Can you work with supervisors?
   - Can you handle criticism?
   - Can you maintain social relationships?

3. **Concentrate, persist, or maintain pace**
   - Can you focus on tasks?
   - Can you complete tasks on time?
   - Can you work at a consistent pace?

4. **Adapt or manage oneself**
   - Can you handle changes in routine?
   - Can you manage your emotions?
   - Can you take care of yourself?

### Key Talking Points

**DO say:**
- "I cannot process verbal instructions with background noise"
- "I need 4 hours between medications or I lose cognitive function"
- "I cannot sustain focus on a single task for more than 20 minutes without a break"
- "Unexpected schedule changes cause shutdown episodes lasting hours"
- "I built an 1,888-line operating system just to manage my daily medications and capacity — that's how much support I need to function"
- "I cannot filter sensory input — when overwhelmed, I shut down completely"
- "I cannot read social cues or understand implied communication"
- "I cannot manage my time without external structure (The Scope enforces medication timing)"
- "I cannot handle criticism or conflict without severe emotional dysregulation"
- "I cannot adapt to changes in routine without days of recovery"

**DON'T say:**
- "I'm trying my best" (they don't care about effort)
- "On good days I can..." (they evaluate worst days)
- "I think I could..." (they evaluate what you actually do)
- "It's hard but I manage" (minimizing doesn't help your case)

### Sample Responses
