## Phase 2: Georgia Nonprofit Formation (Weeks 2-4)

### Step 1: Name Reservation
- **Cost:** $30 online
- **Portal:** sos.ga.gov (eCorp system)
- **Requirement:** Must include corporate designator ("Corporation," "Incorporated," or abbreviation)
- **Action:** Search database → Reserve name

### Step 2: Prepare Organizational Documents
**Required but not filed:**
- [ ] Bylaws (see template below)
- [ ] Conflict of Interest Policy (see template below)

**IRS-Required Clauses for Articles:**
1. **Exclusive charitable purpose clause**
2. **Dissolution clause** (assets to another 501(c)(3))
3. **Private inurement prohibition**

### Step 3: File Articles of Incorporation
- **Cost:** $100 online ($110 by mail)
- **Portal:** sos.ga.gov
- **Required Information:**
  - Registered agent name and physical street address
  - Principal office mailing address
  - Whether corporation will have members
  - **CRITICAL:** Include all three IRS-required clauses above

### Step 4: Publish Notice of Incorporation
- **Cost:** ~$40 (paid directly to newspaper)
- **Timeline:** No later than next business day after filing
- **Action:** Contact local newspaper for publication

### Step 5: File Initial Annual Registration
- **Cost:** $50
- **Timeline:** Within 90 days of incorporation
- **Required:** List three principal officers

### Step 6: Obtain EIN
- **Cost:** Free
- **Portal:** irs.gov
- **Timeline:** Immediate online

### Step 7: Apply for 501(c)(3) Status

**Two Paths:**

**Option A: Form 1023-EZ**
- **Cost:** $275
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks
- **Requirements:**
  - Projected gross receipts < $50,000/year
  - Assets < $250,000
- **Limitation:** Some foundations prefer full Form 1023

**Option B: Form 1023 (Recommended for AT nonprofit)**
- **Cost:** $600
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months (up to 12 months during backlogs)
- **Benefit:** Stronger vetting preferred by foundations and grant-makers
- **Retroactive:** If filed within 27 months of formation, exemption retroactive to incorporation date

**Recommendation:** Use Form 1023 for institutional funding credibility, despite higher cost and longer timeline.

### Step 8: Register for Charitable Solicitation
- **Form:** Georgia Form C-100
- **Cost:** $35 filing fee
- **Timeline:** Required before soliciting donations
- **Renewal:** Every 24 months at $20

### Board Requirements
- **Minimum:** 3 unrelated directors (IRS strongly prefers this)
- **Georgia law:** Technically allows 1 director, but nonprofits formed after July 1, 2023 without members need at least 3
- **Residency:** No Georgia residency requirement for directors

### Registered Agent
- **Options:** Commercial service ($38–$150/year) or individual with Georgia physical address
- **Required:** Physical street address (not P.O. Box)

### Annual Compliance
- **Georgia Annual Registration:** January 1 - April 1 each year ($50)
- **Form 990 Series:** 15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end
  - Under $50,000 gross receipts: Form 990-N e-Postcard (free)
  - **Warning:** Missing three consecutive years triggers automatic revocation

### Sales Tax
**Georgia does NOT provide broad sales tax exemption to nonprofits.** Only specific categories (hospitals, orphanages, schools) qualify. Assistive technology nonprofit would pay standard sales tax on purchases.

**State income tax exemption:** Automatic upon receiving federal determination letter.

## Phase 3: FDA Regulatory Pathway

### 510(k) Exemption Confirmation
**Critical Finding:** Powered communication systems are 510(k)-exempt under **21 CFR § 890.3710** (exempt since November 3, 1998).

**Classification:** Class II device, but 510(k)-exempt.

**Coverage:** Speech generating devices, video picture/word screens, specialized communication hardware. **ESP32-based AAC device squarely fits this classification.**

### What You Still Must Do (Even With Exemption)

1. **Register Establishment with FDA**
   - **Fee:** $11,423 (FY2026)
   - **Waiver:** Businesses with ≤$1 million gross receipts can apply for financial hardship waiver
   - **Timeline:** Annual registration

2. **List Device with FDA**
   - **Fee:** No fee
   - **Requirement:** Mandatory

3. **Implement Quality Management System**
   - **Standard:** 21 CFR Part 820
   - **Requirement:** Full Design Controls (§ 820.30) — mandatory for Class II devices regardless of 510(k) exemption
   - **Components:**
     - Design and development planning
     - Design input
     - Design output
     - Design review
     - Design verification
     - Design validation
     - Design transfer
     - Design changes
     - Design history file

4. **Comply with Labeling Requirements**
   - **Standard:** 21 CFR Part 801
   - **Requirements:** Device labeling, instructions for use, warnings

5. **Medical Device Reporting**
   - **Standard:** 21 CFR Part 803
   - **Requirement:** Report adverse events to FDA

6. **EMC Testing**
   - **Standard:** IEC 60601-1-2 (for electrically powered devices)
   - **Tests Required:**
     - Radiated emissions
     - Conducted emissions
     - ESD immunity
     - RF immunity

### Critical Caveat on Exemption
Under § 890.9, a 510(k) IS required if:
- Device has different intended use than legally marketed devices in that generic type, OR
- Device operates using different fundamental scientific technology

**Standard AAC device using ESP32 for text-to-speech or symbol-based communication:** ✅ Comfortably within exemption

**Device using brain-computer interfaces, novel biosensors, or AI-based diagnostic functions:** ⚠️ Might trigger requirement

### ESP32-Specific Considerations

**COTS Component:** ESP32 is commercial off-the-shelf — doesn't need separate FDA clearance. However, finished device must document ESP32 firmware/SDK as **SOUP (Software of Unknown Provenance)** per IEC 62304.

**Requirements:**
- Document ESP32 firmware version
- Document known anomalies
- Risk analysis
- Cybersecurity risk assessment (per FDA 2023 cybersecurity guidance) due to connectivity features

**WiFi/Bluetooth Radios:**
- EMC testing
- RF shielding
- FCC Part 15 compliance

**ESP-IDF (FreeRTOS-based):**
- Document version
- Known anomalies
- Risk analysis

### If Device is Truly Novel (No Predicate)

**Pathway Options:**

1. **513(g) Request** (Classification Guidance)
   - **Cost:** $3,910 (small business)
   - **Purpose:** Get FDA's classification guidance before filing

2. **Pre-Submission (Q-Sub) Meeting**
   - **Cost:** Free
   - **Purpose:** Discuss regulatory pathway with FDA

3. **510(k) Submission**
   - **Cost:** $6,517 (small business fee, FY2026)
   - **Timeline:** ~90-day FDA review

4. **De Novo Classification**
   - **Cost:** $43,446 (small business)
   - **Timeline:** ~150+ FDA review days

## Phase 4: Intellectual Property Strategy

### Defensive Publication (Recommended for Mission Alignment)

**Cost:** $0–$150 per invention

**Strategy:** Multi-platform defensive publication permanently prevents patenting while keeping technology freely available.

**Platforms:**

1. **Technical Disclosure Commons** (tdcommons.org)
   - **Cost:** Free
   - **Indexed by:** Google Patents
   - **Support:** Google
   - **Status:** Primary formal publication venue

2. **Prior Art Archive** (priorartarchive.org)
   - **Cost:** Free
   - **Operated by:** MIT/Cisco
   - **Status:** Official open-access database to which USPTO directs examiners

3. **ResearchGate or arXiv**
   - **Cost:** Free
   - **Indexed by:** Google Scholar (examiners increasingly use)

4. **Organization Website**
   - **Cost:** Free
   - **Action:** Immediately archive on Internet Archive's Wayback Machine for independent timestamp evidence

5. **IP.com Prior Art Database**
   - **Cost:** ~$109–$150/disclosure
   - **Benefit:** Professional-grade publication with notarization and global patent office distribution
   - **Use:** When budget allows

### What to Include in Defensive Disclosure

**Enablement Standard:** Sufficient detail for someone skilled in the art to replicate the invention.

**Required Elements:**
- Descriptive title with technical keywords
- 150–300 word abstract
- Detailed description of how invention works
- Drawings/diagrams
- Alternative embodiments (critical to prevent design-arounds)
- Concrete use-case examples

**Principle:** More detail and variations = broader prior art footprint.

### Cost Comparison

**Defensive Publication:** $0–$150 per invention

**Utility Patent:**
- Filing + attorney fees: $6,000–$15,000+
- Maintenance fees: 3.5, 7.5, and 11.5 years
- **Nonprofit micro entity discount:** 80% off USPTO fees (Unleashing American Innovators Act of 2022)

**Provisional Patent:**
- USPTO fees: $60–$320
- Attorney fees: $1,500–$3,500
- Duration: 12 months only

### Hybrid Strategy

1. File provisional patent ($60 micro entity fee) to secure filing date and "patent pending" status
2. During 12-month window, decide: full patent prosecution OR defensive publication
3. Complement with open-source licensing:
   - **Apache License 2.0** for software (includes defensive patent termination clause)
   - **CERN Open Hardware License** for physical designs

### Pro Bono Legal Help

**USPTO Patent Pro Bono Program:**
- **Eligibility:** Financially under-resourced inventors (organizations with ≤$175,000 annual budget)
- **Service:** Matches with volunteer patent attorneys

**Georgia PATENTS:**
- **Service:** gapatents.org
- **Coverage:** Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina

## Phase 6: Operational Systems

### Day Theming (Energy Management)

**Traditional time management fails neurodivergent executives** — ignores executive dysfunction, uneven energy distribution, and astronomical context-switching costs (23+ minutes to refocus, projects take 50% longer when interrupted).

**Recommended Structure:**

| Day | Role | Key Activities |
|-----|------|---------------|
| Monday | Engineer | Deep technical work, coding, product development. No meetings, no email until 4 PM. |
| Tuesday | Engineer | Continue building, testing, technical documentation. |
| Wednesday | CEO | Board communications, partner meetings, emails, admin, financial review, strategy. |
| Thursday | Grant writer | Grant research, proposal writing, donor outreach, impact reporting. |
| Friday | Flex + strategy | Overflow tasks, weekly review, next-week planning, professional development. |

**Energy Management Framework:**
- Rate energy 1–10 each morning
- Track patterns over 2–3 weeks to identify "golden hours"
- Protect golden hours absolutely for highest-priority deep work
- Human brain: ~4–5 hours deep focus capacity per day

**For ADHD/Autistic Executives:**
- **Body doubling:** Working alongside another person (even virtually) lowers activation barrier
- **20-minute rule:** "I'll only do this for 20 minutes" defeats paralysis
- **Hyperfocus protection:** Set external alarms to prevent losing entire days
- **Admin hour:** Collect small requests for designated time slot (don't respond in real-time during deep work)

**Time Allocation (First 6 Months):**
- 50% engineering/product development
- 30% fundraising/grant writing
- 20% CEO/operations

**Time Allocation (Year 2):**
- Roughly equal thirds

**Prioritization Cascade:**
- **MoSCoW** (quarterly): Define scope (limit "Must Haves" to 3–5 items)
- **ICE Scoring** (monthly): Impact × Confidence × Ease
- **Eisenhower Matrix** (weekly): Handle urgency
- **"The ONE Thing"** (daily): "What's the one thing I can do such that by doing it, everything else will be easier or unnecessary?"

## Organizational Document Templates

### Articles of Incorporation - IRS Required Clauses

**Clause 1: Exclusive Charitable Purpose**
"The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the 'Code')."

**Clause 2: Dissolution**
"Upon the dissolution of the corporation, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by a court of competent jurisdiction of the county in which the principal office of the corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes."

**Clause 3: Private Inurement Prohibition**
"No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, trustees, officers, or other private persons, except that the corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Article [X] hereof. No substantial part of the activities of the corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office. Notwithstanding any other provision of these articles, the corporation shall not carry on any other activities not permitted to be carried on (a) by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or (b) by a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code."
