# P31 Labs: Application Narrative
## Open-Source Assistive Technology for Neurodivergent Minds

---

### The Problem

Thirty million American adults live with ADHD, autism, or both. The assistive technology available to them was designed for children, by neurotypical developers, sold at clinical prices, and built around deficits rather than differences. An autistic adult navigating a career, a family, and a healthcare system has almost nothing purpose-built for their brain. The existing tools assume you need to be fixed. We build tools that assume you need to be supported.

### The Solution

P31 Labs develops open-source assistive technology across three layers:

**Node One** is an ESP32-S3 wearable device with haptic feedback (DRV2605L motor driver) and LoRa mesh communication. It provides real-time sensory regulation through tactile signals — a gentle pulse when a meeting runs long, a rhythm pattern for task transitions, a vibration sequence that grounds the wearer during sensory overload. For neurodivergent adults, it is an external executive function prosthetic you can hold in your hand.

**The Buffer** is communication processing software that sits between an incoming message and the user's response. It scores message "voltage" — urgency, emotional intensity, cognitive load — and presents it in a processed format the user can engage with at their own pace. For someone whose brain needs 5 seconds to process what their ears heard 5 seconds ago, The Buffer turns the firehose into a garden hose.

**The Centaur Protocol** is our human-AI collaboration framework. It treats the AI as a synthetic body and the human as the biological operator — two halves of one organism. Every tool P31 builds assumes this pairing. The technology extends the human. It does not replace them.

All designs, firmware, and software are open-source. All of it.

### The Founder

I am William R. Johnson, age 40, diagnosed with autism and ADHD at age 39 after sixteen years as a DoD civilian submarine electrician (GS-05 to GS-12). For two decades I worked in one of the highest-sensory, most communication-intensive environments in federal service — and I did it without knowing why everything was so hard. After diagnosis, I could not unknow the cost. I left federal service in May 2025 and began building what should have existed when I needed it.

I have hypoparathyroidism, a permanent endocrine condition since 2003 that disrupts calcium-phosphate metabolism — the same biochemistry that P31 Labs is named after (Phosphorus-31, the NMR-active nucleus in brain energy metabolism). My disability is not separate from the technology. It is the technology's origin.

### Traction

P31 Labs has a live, functional codebase: 100+ React components, 24 frontend libraries, 21 standalone web applications, ESP32 firmware, and a full CI/CD pipeline deployed across two domains (phosphorus31.org, p31ca.org). We have published peer-accessible research on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18627420 — "The Tetrahedron Protocol: A Geometric Framework for Sovereign Communication"). Our Substack newsletter, *The Geodesic Self*, has 33 subscribers with 3,200% 30-day growth and 967 page views. We have an established ORCID (0009-0002-2492-9079) and are actively expanding our publication record.

We are pre-revenue, pre-501(c)(3) determination, and one person deep. What we have is a working product, published research, a growing audience, and an origin story that is also a use case.

### The Ask

Funding accelerates three things: hardware prototyping (first 50 Node One units for user testing), server infrastructure (hosting the software suite and API layer), and the founder's time (P31 Labs has one employee, and he is also the primary user). Every dollar goes to building the tools. The mission is the budget.
