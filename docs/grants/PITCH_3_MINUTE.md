**Multiple Autism Tech Accelerator · Demo Day**

## THE HOOK (20 seconds)
I was diagnosed autistic and ADHD at 39, after 16 years of unaccommodated federal service as a DoD civilian submarine electrician. I maintained nuclear submarine electrical systems for two decades — safety-critical hardware where failure means death. I did this work while undiagnosed, unaccommodated, and unaware that my brain worked differently.

The diagnosis came after I left federal service in May 2025. I realized the tools I needed to function didn't exist. So I started building them.

## THE PROBLEM (40 seconds)
The assistive technology market has a fundamental mismatch: most tools are designed by neurotypical people for neurotypical assumptions about disability. This creates three critical gaps:

**First, the sensory regulation gap.** Existing tools assume users can filter sensory input. Autistic people cannot. When overwhelm triggers shutdown, verbal communication fails. There's no single-purpose device that works when everything else fails.

**Second, the communication triage gap.** Neurodivergent people are vulnerable to coercion, manipulation, and high-pressure communication. There's no system that scans messages for voltage and holds dangerous content until the operator has capacity.

**Third, the executive function tracking gap.** Existing medication trackers don't account for drug interactions. Existing task managers don't account for spoon economy. There's no unified dashboard that tracks all of this.

When systems fail — divorce, job loss, benefits disruption — neurodivergent people lose their ground reference. There's no technology layer to catch them.

## THE SOLUTION (60 seconds)
P31 Labs builds assistive technology for neurodivergent minds. Our stack has three layers, one mesh:

**Node One** is hardware — an ESP32-S3 handheld device with DRV2605L haptic feedback and LoRa mesh networking. It works offline, no cloud dependency. This isn't a phone app; it's a purpose-built tool for sensory regulation and emergency communication when verbal communication fails.

**The Buffer** is software — a communication triage system that scans incoming messages for coercion patterns (urgency, shame, false authority). It assigns voltage scores (0-10) and holds high-voltage messages until the operator is ready. It automatically generates ADA accommodation documentation.

**The Scope** is a daily operating dashboard — 1,888 lines of production code currently running in Google Apps Script. It tracks spoons (executive function capacity), medications with 4-hour calcium-stimulant gap enforcement, coherence monitoring, and system alerts. This is in daily production use.

Everything is open source (Apache 2.0) and defensively published to Zenodo. The architecture follows Delta topology — mesh networking, no single point of failure.
