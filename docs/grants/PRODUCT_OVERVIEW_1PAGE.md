## PRODUCT: THREE-LAYER ASSISTIVE TECHNOLOGY STACK

### Node One (Hardware)
**ESP32-S3 handheld device** with DRV2605L haptic feedback and LoRa mesh networking. Works offline, no cloud dependency. Purpose-built for sensory regulation and emergency communication when verbal communication fails.

**Status:** ESP32-S3 prototype functional. DRV2605L haptics working. LoRa mesh (Meshtastic) operational. Emergency messaging ("MELTDOWN", "NONVERBAL") implemented.

**Regulatory:** Class II assistive medical device pathway.

### The Buffer (Software — Communication Layer)
**Communication voltage scanner** that analyzes incoming messages for coercion patterns (urgency, shame, false authority, threats). Assigns voltage scores (0-10). Holds high-voltage messages (≥6) automatically. Generates ADA accommodation documentation.

**Status:** Gemini AI integration complete. Voltage detection functional. Auto-hold system operational.

### The Scope (Software — Dashboard)
**Daily operating dashboard** — 1,888 lines of production code (Google Apps Script). Tracks spoons (executive function capacity), medications with 4-hour calcium-stimulant gap enforcement, coherence monitoring, ping grid, and system alerts.

**Status:** Production — daily driver since late 2025. Six integrated systems: medication tracker, spoon economy, coherence monitor, ping system, alert manager, accommodation log.

## PROBLEM
Most assistive technology is designed BY neurotypical people FOR neurotypical assumptions about disability. This creates three gaps:

1. **Sensory regulation gap:** No offline device for sensory overwhelm when verbal communication fails.
2. **Communication triage gap:** No system to protect neurodivergent people from coercion and high-pressure communication.
3. **Executive function tracking gap:** No unified dashboard that accounts for drug interactions and spoon economy.

When systems fail (divorce, job loss, benefits disruption), neurodivergent people lose their ground reference. There's no technology layer to catch them.

**Secondary:** Families with neurodivergent children (ages 6-17) seeking tools that respect autonomy while providing safety nets.

**Tertiary:** Schools and workplaces implementing ADA accommodations.

**Market:** 1 in 36 children diagnosed autistic, plus late-diagnosed adults. Underserved market (most AT designed for neurotypical users). Growing (diagnosis rates increasing).
