# Articles of Incorporation - IRS Required Clauses

**For Georgia 501(c)(3) Nonprofit Organizations**

---

## Overview

Georgia's standard Articles of Incorporation template does **not** include the three IRS-required clauses for 501(c)(3) tax exemption. You must manually add these clauses to your Articles before filing with the Georgia Secretary of State.

**Important:** These clauses must appear in your Articles of Incorporation. They cannot be added later through bylaws alone.

---

## The Three Required Clauses

### 1. Exclusive Charitable Purpose Clause

**Purpose:** Ensures the organization is organized exclusively for charitable purposes.

**Required Language:**

```
The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), including but not limited to:

[Insert your specific charitable purposes here, for example:]
- Developing, manufacturing, and distributing assistive technology devices and software for individuals with disabilities
- Providing education, training, and support services related to assistive technology
- Conducting research and development in assistive technology
- Advocating for accessibility and inclusion in technology

Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles, the Corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.
```

**Where to Add:** In the "Purpose" section of your Articles of Incorporation.

---

### 2. Private Inurement Prohibition Clause

**Purpose:** Prohibits the distribution of earnings to private individuals (insiders).

**Required Language:**

```
No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, trustees, directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in these Articles.
```

**Where to Add:** In a separate section, typically after the Purpose section, or combined with the nonprofit status section.

---

### 3. Dissolution Clause

**Purpose:** Ensures that upon dissolution, assets go to another 501(c)(3) organization, not to private individuals.

**Required Language:**

```
Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, the Board of Directors shall, after paying or making provision for the payment of all of the liabilities of the Corporation, dispose of all of the assets of the Corporation exclusively for the purposes of the Corporation in such manner, or to such organization or organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, or scientific purposes as shall at the time qualify as an exempt organization or organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, as the Board of Directors shall determine. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by a court of competent jurisdiction of the county in which the principal office of the Corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.
```

**Where to Add:** In a separate "Dissolution" section of your Articles of Incorporation.

## Complete Example: Articles of Incorporation Section

Here's how these clauses should appear in your Articles of Incorporation:

```markdown
## ARTICLE II: PURPOSE

The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), including but not limited to:

(a) Developing, manufacturing, and distributing assistive technology devices and software for individuals with disabilities, with a particular focus on communication devices for individuals with autism and neurodivergent conditions;

(b) Providing education, training, and support services related to assistive technology;

(c) Conducting research and development in assistive technology;

(d) Advocating for accessibility and inclusion in technology;

(e) Any other charitable, educational, or scientific purposes consistent with Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles, the Corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

## ARTICLE III: NONPROFIT STATUS AND PRIVATE INUREMENT

The Corporation shall not have or issue shares of stock. No dividend shall be paid, and no part of the income or assets of the Corporation shall be distributed to its members, directors, or officers, except that the Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and may make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purposes.

No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, trustees, directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Article II hereof.

## ARTICLE IV: POLITICAL ACTIVITIES

No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

## ARTICLE V: DISSOLUTION

Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, the Board of Directors shall, after paying or making provision for the payment of all of the liabilities of the Corporation, dispose of all of the assets of the Corporation exclusively for the purposes of the Corporation in such manner, or to such organization or organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, or scientific purposes as shall at the time qualify as an exempt organization or organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, as the Board of Directors shall determine. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by a court of competent jurisdiction of the county in which the principal office of the Corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.
```

## Filing Checklist

Before filing your Articles of Incorporation with the Georgia Secretary of State:

- [ ] Verify all three IRS clauses are included
- [ ] Ensure the language matches exactly (or consult with an attorney)
- [ ] Review the entire document for consistency
- [ ] Have the document reviewed by an attorney (recommended)
- [ ] File with Georgia Secretary of State ($100 filing fee)
- [ ] Keep a copy for your records and for the 501(c)(3) application

---

## Why These Clauses Matter

The IRS requires these clauses in your Articles of Incorporation (not just in your bylaws) because:

1. **Articles are harder to change** - They require state filing and sometimes court approval, making them more permanent than bylaws.

2. **Legal protection** - Having these clauses in the Articles provides stronger legal protection against challenges to your tax-exempt status.

3. **IRS requirement** - The IRS specifically looks for these clauses in Articles of Incorporation when reviewing Form 1023 or Form 1023-EZ applications.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:** Rely on bylaws alone - the clauses must be in the Articles.

❌ **Don't:** Use vague language - be specific about charitable purposes.

❌ **Don't:** Omit any of the three clauses - all are required.

❌ **Don't:** File without reviewing - have an attorney review before filing.

✅ **Do:** Include all three clauses in your Articles.

✅ **Do:** Use the exact language or consult with an attorney for variations.

✅ **Do:** Keep copies of your filed Articles for the 501(c)(3) application.
