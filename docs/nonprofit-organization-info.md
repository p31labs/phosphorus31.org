## Next Steps

1. **Review Documents**
   - Review bylaws and conflict of interest policy
   - Fill in placeholder dates and officer names
   - Customize any sections as needed (e.g., term lengths, meeting notice periods)

2. **Articles of Incorporation**
   - Use Georgia's template from sos.ga.gov
   - Add the three IRS clauses using `docs/articles-of-incorporation-irs-clauses.md`
   - Have an attorney review (recommended)

3. **Board Adoption**
   - Schedule initial Board meeting
   - Adopt bylaws and conflict of interest policy
   - Sign and date both documents
   - Keep signed copies in corporate records

4. **Filing**
   - File Articles of Incorporation with Georgia Secretary of State ($100)
   - Arrange newspaper publication (~$40)
   - File initial annual registration ($50)
   - Obtain EIN from IRS (free)

5. **501(c)(3) Application**
   - Complete Form 1023-EZ ($275) or Form 1023 ($600)
   - Attach filed Articles of Incorporation
   - Attach adopted bylaws
   - Attach adopted conflict of interest policy
   - Submit to IRS

## Placeholders to Fill In

Before finalizing documents, fill in:

- `[DATE]` - Date of adoption
- `[PRESIDENT NAME]` - President's name
- `[SECRETARY NAME]` - Secretary's name
- `[ONE/TWO/THREE]` - Director term length (in bylaws)
- `[ONE/TWO]` - Officer term length (in bylaws)
- `[24 HOURS/48 HOURS]` - Special meeting notice period (in bylaws)
- `[SHALL/SHALL NOT]` - Whether corporation will have members (in bylaws)
- `[CALENDAR YEAR/JANUARY 1 - DECEMBER 31]` - Fiscal year (in bylaws)
