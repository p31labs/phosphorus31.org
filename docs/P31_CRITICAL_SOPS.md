## SOP 3: Georgia 501(c)(3) Nonprofit Formation (Target: Begin by March 1, Complete by March 12)

### What This Involves and the Total Cost

Forming a Georgia nonprofit corporation and obtaining federal 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status involves **four sequential processes**: (1) state incorporation ($100), (2) newspaper publication ($40), (3) EIN application (free), and (4) IRS Form 1023-EZ ($275). After IRS approval, you'll need Georgia charitable solicitation registration ($35) and the initial annual registration with the Georgia Secretary of State ($30). **Total minimum cost: $480.** The IRS is currently processing Form 1023-EZ applications within approximately **22 days** for straightforward cases, meaning you could have full 501(c)(3) status by mid-April if you file by mid-March.

### The 3-Director Requirement You Must Address First

**Georgia law (O.C.G.A. § 14-3-803, effective July 1, 2023) requires a minimum of 3 directors** for nonprofit corporations without members formed after July 1, 2023. Additionally, the IRS strongly prefers **3 unrelated directors** (not related by blood, marriage, or business relationship) for 501(c)(3) approval. Georgia also now requires **three separate officers** — a CEO (or President), Secretary, and CFO (or Treasurer) — and the **CEO and Secretary cannot be the same person** (O.C.G.A. § 14-3-840(e)).

This means The Operator needs to recruit **at least 2 additional people** before filing articles of incorporation. These individuals will serve as directors and can also fill the required officer positions. One person can hold multiple officer titles (except the CEO/Secretary restriction), but each director seat must be a separate individual.

### Potential Pitfalls and Mitigations

**Pitfall 1: Using Georgia's template articles without IRS provisions.** The Georgia SOS explicitly warns: *"The Georgia template does NOT meet Internal Revenue Service requirements for obtaining 501(c)(3) status."* Always include all five IRS-required clauses from Phase B.

**Pitfall 2: Forgetting the newspaper publication deadline.** You must send the notice to the legal organ **within 1 business day** of filing articles. Set an alarm for the day you file. Have the notice text pre-written (Phase D, step D2) so you can send it immediately.

**Pitfall 3: Not having 3 directors.** This is a hard legal requirement for post-July-2023 nonprofits without members. The IRS also independently prefers 3+ unrelated directors. Start recruiting now — this is often the biggest bottleneck for solo founders.

**Pitfall 4: Trying to complete Pay.gov forms in multiple sessions.** Both the EIN application and Form 1023-EZ cannot be saved mid-session. Prepare all information in a document first, then complete each form in one focused session. Consider using a body double for accountability.

**Pitfall 5: Missing the 27-month window for retroactive exemption.** File Form 1023-EZ within 27 months of the end of your incorporation month. For a March 2026 incorporation, this means filing by June 2028. But file immediately — there's no benefit to waiting.

**Pitfall 6: CEO and Secretary being the same person.** Georgia's 2023 amendments prohibit this. Assign these roles to two different people from your board of 3+.
