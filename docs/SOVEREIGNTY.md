## What is Sovereignty?

**Sovereignty** in P31 means **data ownership, self-hosted services, and local-first operation**. It's the principle that **you control your data, your hardware, and your keys**.

### Core Principle

**"The protocol, not the vendor."**

- Your data lives on your devices first
- Services run on hardware you own
- No vendor lock-in
- Encrypted by default
- You control the keys

## Sovereign Architecture

### Local-First

**Data lives on your devices first.**

- Primary storage: Local (your devices)
- Cloud sync: Optional, encrypted, end-to-end
- Offline-first: Works without internet
- You control: What syncs, when, and where

### Self-Hosted

**Services run on hardware you own.**

- **Sovereign Life OS:** 72 self-hosted services
- Replaces $4,000-7,000+/yr in SaaS subscriptions
- Full data sovereignty
- No vendor dependencies

### Hybrid Operation

**Cloud when connected. Local when sovereign. Hybrid when in between.**

- **Connected:** Use cloud services when needed
- **Sovereign:** Full local operation when desired
- **Hybrid:** Mix of both, you choose

## Sovereign Principles

### 1. Data Ownership
- **You own your data** — Not stored in vendor databases
- **You control access** — Encrypted, local-first
- **You decide what syncs** — Optional cloud sync

### 2. Hardware Ownership
- **You own the hardware** — Self-hosted services
- **You control the infrastructure** — No vendor dependencies
- **You maintain sovereignty** — Full control

### 3. Protocol, Not Vendor
- **The protocol doesn't change** — Even if vendors do
- **No vendor lock-in** — Switch vendors, keep protocol
- **Open standards** — Interoperable, portable

### 4. Encrypted by Default
- **Privacy is built in** — Not a feature, the foundation
- **Type-level encryption** — EncryptedBlob for all content
- **Zero-knowledge** — Where possible, system doesn't need to know

### 5. Local-First
- **Works offline** — No internet required
- **Fast local access** — No cloud latency
- **Privacy protected** — Data stays local
