Research the neurological effects of calcium imbalance, specifically the "calcium paradox" where calcium deficiency causes neuronal hyperexcitability. I need:

1. The basic neuroscience of how neurons fire — resting potential, action potential, the role of ions (Na+, K+, Ca2+). Explain for a non-scientist.
2. How extracellular calcium stabilizes voltage-gated sodium channels — the "screening" effect on the voltage sensor
3. The paradox: why LESS calcium leads to MORE neural firing (not less). Make this intuitive.
4. Clinical manifestations of hypocalcemia: tetany, paresthesias, Chvostek's sign, Trousseau's sign, seizures, cognitive dysfunction
5. The emotional and psychiatric manifestations — anxiety, irritability, "brain fog," difficulty concentrating. How these get misdiagnosed as psychiatric conditions.
6. The analogy to electrical engineering: stripping insulation from wires, lowering the activation threshold, the nervous system as a circuit
7. How calcium regulation works normally — parathyroid hormone, vitamin D, the feedback loop
8. What happens when this system fails (hypoparathyroidism) — the permanent state of "voltage instability"
9. Treatment and management — what works, what doesn't, quality of life implications
10. Any patient narratives or first-person accounts of living with hypoparathyroidism and cognitive symptoms

I'm writing a personal essay about living with hypoparathyroidism for 22 years (since age 17) and being an electrical engineer who finally understood his own condition through engineering metaphors. The piece needs to be scientifically accurate but emotionally resonant.
