## MISSION 1: Grant & Funding Landscape
**Priority:** 🔴 Supports Swarm Zeta
**Paste this:**

```
Research current grant opportunities for a Georgia-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit developing open-source assistive technology for neurodivergent individuals (ADHD + autism). The organization is called P31 Labs. It builds hardware (ESP32-S3 wearable), software (communication processing tools), and protocols (human-AI collaboration).

I need:
1. A list of 10-15 active grants or funding programs that accept applications from:
   - Assistive technology developers
   - Disability-focused nonprofits
   - Open-source hardware/software projects
   - Neurodivergence-specific programs
   - SBIR/STTR for assistive devices

2. For each grant, provide:
   - Program name and sponsoring organization
   - Application deadline (or if rolling)
   - Typical award amount
   - Application URL
   - Whether the application is digital-only (no in-person pitch required)
   - Fit assessment: how well does it match an early-stage, single-founder, pre-revenue nonprofit with an extensive codebase but no commercial product yet?

3. Specifically look for:
   - NIDILRR (National Institute on Disability, Independent Living, and Rehabilitation Research)
   - Administration for Community Living grants
   - Christopher & Dana Reeve Foundation Quality of Life grants
   - Mozilla Foundation open source grants
   - Google.org Impact Challenge for disabilities
   - Any Georgia-specific nonprofit technology grants
   - Disability Innovation Fund (DOE)
   - AT&T Believe program or similar corporate AT funding

4. Exclude: programs requiring in-person pitches, demo days, or cohort residencies. Digital-first applications only.

Format as a table with columns: Program | Org | Deadline | Amount | URL | Digital-Only? | Fit (1-5)
```

## MISSION 3: SSA Disability Examination — What to Expect
**Priority:** 🔴 Supports Swarm Alpha
**Paste this:**

```
I have two SSA (Social Security Administration) disability examinations coming up:
1. February 20, 2026 — Telehealth psychiatric examination
2. February 26, 2026 — In-person medical examination in Brunswick, GA

My conditions: ADHD (diagnosed 2025), Autism Spectrum Disorder (diagnosed 2025), Hypoparathyroidism (since 2003).

I need detailed research on:

1. What happens during an SSA consultative psychiatric examination (telehealth)?
   - Typical format and duration
   - What questions do they ask?
   - What are they evaluating? (specific functional areas)
   - What should I bring or have ready?
   - Common mistakes applicants make

2. What happens during an SSA consultative medical/physical examination?
   - Typical format and duration
   - What physical tests do they perform?
   - How do they assess ADHD/autism in adults?
   - How do they assess hypoparathyroidism?
   - What documentation should I bring?

3. How does SSA evaluate disability for ADHD + Autism in adults specifically?
   - Which Blue Book listings are relevant?
   - What functional limitations matter most?
   - How do they assess "residual functional capacity" for cognitive/mental conditions?
   - What's the difference between how they evaluate physical vs. mental limitations?

4. What should I NOT do?
   - Common behaviors that hurt applications
   - Things that make examiners skeptical
   - Specific to neurodivergent applicants: masking concerns (appearing "fine" during exam while actually struggling)

5. Masking warning: As a late-diagnosed autistic adult (40 years old, diagnosed at 39), I have decades of masking behavior. How do I communicate genuine functional limitations when my default mode is appearing competent? This is the central challenge.

Format as a preparation guide I can review the night before each exam.
```
