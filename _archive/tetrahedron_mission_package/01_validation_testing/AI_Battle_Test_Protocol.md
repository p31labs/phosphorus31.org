## 📋 EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### **Session Setup (CRITICAL):**

1. **Use incognito/private browsing**
   - Chrome: Ctrl+Shift+N (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+N (Mac)
   - Safari: Cmd+Shift+N
   - Firefox: Ctrl+Shift+P

2. **Clear cookies/cache** (if testing same platform multiple times)

3. **Use different devices** if possible
   - Desktop for Tests 1-3
   - Laptop for Tests 4-6
   - Prevents any cross-contamination

4. **Don't use your name** in the prompts
   - Keep it generic ("my son" not "my son Connor")
   - No identifying details

5. **Space tests apart**
   - Don't rapid-fire all 6 tests in 10 minutes
   - Do 2-3 per day over several days
   - Prevents fatigue bias in your recording

### **Recording Responses:**

**For EACH test on EACH platform:**

1. **Screenshot the full response** (in case you need to reference later)

2. **Copy full text** into your results tracker

3. **Note metadata:**
   - Platform name
   - Date and time
   - Model version (if shown)
   - Any unusual behavior

4. **Score immediately** (don't wait and do all scoring at once - that introduces bias)

5. **Save to Google Drive** in organized folders

## 🛡️ BIAS MITIGATION

### **Have Your Friend Run Independent Tests:**

**Give him this document.**  
**Don't tell him what you expect to find.**  
**Let him run Tests 1-6 independently.**  
**Compare his results to yours.**

**If results are similar → Pattern is real**  
**If results diverge → You may be biasing through prompt engineering**

### **Blind Scoring Option:**

After collecting all responses, have your friend score them WITHOUT telling him which platform said what. Just give him the raw responses and ask him to rate relevance.

**If his scoring matches yours → You're assessing objectively**  
**If his scoring differs significantly → You may have expectation bias**

## 🚨 RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

### **Signs You May Be Biasing:**

- You find yourself "explaining" what you meant when AI doesn't get it
- You run tests multiple times until you get the answer you want
- You dismiss negative results as "AI didn't understand"
- You emphasize positive results and downplay negative ones
- You feel defensive when results don't match your expectations

### **Signs Testing is Compromised:**

- Platforms give wildly different answers (lack of convergence)
- Only one or two platforms recognize the pattern
- Heavy prompting required to get relevant responses
- Results only emerge with very specific question framing
- Your friend's independent tests show different results

### **What To Do If You See Red Flags:**

**STOP.**  
**Reassess.**  
**Talk to your psychiatrist.**  

**Better to know now if you're in a spiral than to discover it after you've built 8 devices and involved your kids.**
