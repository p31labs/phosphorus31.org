### MY CONDITIONS

**Autism Spectrum Disorder** — Diagnosed 2025, age 39. Masked for 16+ years in federal service. Late diagnosis means decades of unaccommodated disability.

**ADHD** — Diagnosed 2025, age 39. Combined type. Executive function deficits affect every area of daily living.

**Hypoparathyroidism** — Since 2003 (22 years). Thyroid surgery complication. Disrupts calcium-phosphate metabolism. Causes fatigue, muscle spasms, cognitive fog. Daily medication: calcitriol, calcium carbonate.

### WHAT BROKE AND WHEN

16 years DoD civilian service (GS-05 → GS-12). Submarine electrical systems. I survived by masking — performing neurotypical communication, suppressing sensory distress, white-knuckling executive function. After diagnosis at 39, the mask became unsustainable. I could not unknow what I now knew about why everything was so hard.

### DAILY LIMITATIONS (BE SPECIFIC)

- **Task initiation:** I can sit for hours knowing I need to do something and be physically unable to start. Not unwilling. Unable.
- **Sequencing:** Multi-step tasks (cooking, cleaning, bills) require written checklists or they collapse.
- **Sensory:** Fluorescent lights, crowd noise, unexpected touch cause shutdown. Recovery takes hours.
- **Communication:** Processing delay — I hear words but meaning arrives seconds later. I rehearse conversations before making phone calls.
- **Energy:** Finite daily capacity. One appointment can consume an entire day's function.
- **Self-care:** Skipped meals, irregular sleep, hygiene lapses during low-capacity periods.

### IF THEY ASK ABOUT WORK CAPACITY

I can hyperfocus on complex technical problems in bursts. I cannot sustain 8 hours of consistent output. I cannot maintain attendance. I cannot manage the social/communication demands of a workplace. The difference between what I can do in a burst and what I can do reliably is the disability.
