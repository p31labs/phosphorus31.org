# The Floating Neutral: Why Everything Broke
### What submarine electricians know about losing your ground

---

In electrical engineering, a Wye topology is a system where three phases connect to a single neutral point. Every circuit in the system references that neutral to stay balanced. The voltages stay predictable. The equipment stays alive. The lights stay on.

When the neutral breaks — when it "floats" — everything downstream becomes unpredictable. Voltages swing wildly between phases. Equipment that expects 120 volts gets 180. Equipment that expects 180 gets 60. Things that shouldn't burn, burn. Things that should work, don't. And the terrifying part is that the system can look fine from the outside. The lights might even still be on. But the ground is gone, and everything is running on borrowed time.

I was a submarine electrician for sixteen years. I know what a floating neutral looks like. I've traced them through cable runs in the bellies of nuclear submarines, where a ground fault isn't an inconvenience — it's a survival event. I learned to trust instruments over appearances. I learned that the most dangerous failures are the ones that don't look broken.

In 2025, my neutral broke.

---

I was diagnosed with autism and ADHD at thirty-nine. The same year, I left federal service after sixteen years. The same year, my marriage ended. The same year, I entered family court. The same year, I filed for disability.

Everything floated.

The neutral — the single reference point that every other part of my life was balanced against — turned out to be a mask I'd been wearing since childhood. A mask I didn't know was a mask until a psychologist told me it had a name. For thirty-nine years, I had been referencing my life against a ground that wasn't there.

I got diagnosed, and the mask didn't come off. It shattered. You can't consciously perform something you've just learned was unconscious. You can't unknow the cost. Every meeting, every phone call, every trip to the grocery store — I could now see the energy it took. And once I could see it, I couldn't afford it anymore.

That's what a floating neutral does. It doesn't destroy things instantly. It makes the costs visible. And once they're visible, you realize the system was never balanced. It was just compensating.

---

The instinct when a neutral breaks is to fix it. Reconnect the ground. Restore the reference. Go back to how things were.

I tried that. It doesn't work. Once you know the neutral was always floating — that the balance was always an illusion maintained by your own unsustainable effort — you can't just reconnect it. The ground wasn't ground. It was you, holding the wire, pretending to be ground. For thirty-nine years.

So I stopped trying to fix the Wye.

I started building Delta.

---

In a Delta topology, there is no neutral. Every phase connects directly to every other phase. The system is self-referencing. If one connection fails, the other two still hold. It's inherently more resilient because it doesn't depend on a single point of balance.

In structural engineering, the Delta is a triangle — the simplest rigid shape. Three sides, each supporting the other two. A triangle can't collapse without breaking a member. A Wye collapses the moment its center fails.

That's what P31 Labs is. I didn't start a nonprofit because I had a business plan. I started it because I needed to rebuild my life as a Delta topology. Every connection supports every other connection. The technology I build for neurodivergent brains is the same technology I need to function. The published research validates the technical work. The technical work is the disability accommodation. The disability is the origin of the research.

Three sides. Each supporting the other two. No neutral required.

---

If you're reading this and your neutral just broke — if the ground you've been referencing your whole life just turned out to be floating — I want you to know something.

You are not broken. The topology was wrong.

You don't need to find a new neutral. You need to rebuild as Delta. Find three things that support each other. Make them rigid. Let everything else float.

The lights might flicker for a while. That's normal. You're rewiring under load.

But Delta doesn't need a neutral. And neither do you.
