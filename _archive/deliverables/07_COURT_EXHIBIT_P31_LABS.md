# What P31 Labs Is and Why It Matters

P31 Labs is a registered Georgia nonprofit corporation founded by William R. Johnson in 2026. Its purpose is to build free, publicly available technology tools for adults whose brains process information differently — specifically, adults with autism and ADHD. The organization is named after Phosphorus-31, an atom involved in how the brain produces and uses energy, reflecting its scientific foundation.

Mr. Johnson founded P31 Labs after being diagnosed with autism and ADHD at age 39, following sixteen years of federal civilian service as a submarine electrician for the Department of Defense. During those sixteen years, he advanced from GS-05 to GS-12 — work that required complex technical skill, sustained effort, and significant personal cost that he could not identify until his diagnosis. After diagnosis, he directed that same technical expertise toward building the tools he needed but could not find.

The organization builds three things. First, a small handheld device that provides gentle vibrations to help the wearer stay focused, manage sensory overload, and transition between tasks — similar in concept to how a hearing aid helps someone hear, except this helps with attention and sensory processing. Second, software that takes incoming messages (texts, emails, notifications) and presents them in a calmer, more manageable format, giving the user time to process before responding. Third, a complete set of web-based tools — over twenty small applications — for tracking daily energy, organizing tasks, and managing the practical demands of daily life when executive function is impaired.

All of this technology is freely available. The designs are published openly online so that anyone can build, modify, or improve them. The software runs on two public websites: phosphorus31.org (the organization's informational site) and p31ca.org (the working applications). Mr. Johnson has also published a formal research paper through Zenodo, an academic repository operated by CERN, establishing the scientific framework behind the technology.

P31 Labs demonstrates three things relevant to this proceeding. First, that Mr. Johnson possesses genuine technical and intellectual capability, directed toward serving others with similar disabilities. Second, that despite the functional limitations associated with his diagnosed conditions, he has channeled his expertise into a productive, community-serving organization — the kind of structured, self-directed, meaningful work his disability allows in ways that traditional employment does not. Third, that P31 Labs represents a stable, purposeful commitment — not a passing interest, but an organization with published research, a working product, a growing readership, and a clear mission.

The existence of P31 Labs does not contradict Mr. Johnson's disability. It illustrates it. The technology he builds is the accommodation he requires. The nonprofit is both his contribution and his prosthetic.
