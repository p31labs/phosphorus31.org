# FUNCTIONAL LIMITATION STATEMENT
## William R. Johnson | SSA Disability Application
## Conditions: Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD (Combined), Hypoparathyroidism

---

| Domain | Specific Limitation | Impact on Daily Function | Duration/Frequency |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------------|--------------------|
| **EXECUTIVE FUNCTION** | Task initiation paralysis — inability to begin known tasks despite awareness of urgency | Bills go unpaid, appointments are missed, meals are skipped. Requires external prompting (alarms, checklists, another person) to initiate routine activities. | Daily. Worsens with fatigue, sensory load, or unstructured time. |
| | Working memory deficits — cannot hold multi-step instructions | Loses track mid-task. Starts cooking, forgets stove is on. Begins a form, loses context by page 2. Requires written step-by-step guides for tasks most adults perform automatically. | Daily. Affects every multi-step activity. |
| | Time blindness — impaired perception of time passage | Cannot estimate how long tasks take. Chronically late or obsessively early (compensatory anxiety). Cannot self-regulate pace of work without external timers. | Constant. Lifelong, worsened post-diagnosis awareness. |
| | Decision fatigue — overwhelmed by choices | Routine decisions (what to eat, what to wear, which task first) consume disproportionate cognitive resources. Leads to avoidance, shutdown, or defaulting to no action. | Daily. Cumulative throughout the day. |
| **SENSORY PROCESSING** | Auditory hypersensitivity — inability to filter background noise | Cannot function in environments with overlapping conversations, HVAC noise, or fluorescent light hum. Grocery stores, offices, waiting rooms cause acute distress. Required noise-canceling headphones for final years of DoD service. | Constant. Managed by environmental avoidance. |
| | Tactile defensiveness — aversive response to unexpected touch, textures, tags | Clothing selection severely limited. Cannot wear certain fabrics. Unexpected physical contact causes startle/withdrawal. Haircuts, dental work, medical exams require significant preparation. | Constant. Compensated by rigid clothing routines. |
| | Visual processing overload — difficulty with bright/flickering light | Fluorescent lighting causes headaches, fatigue, and cognitive degradation within 30-60 minutes. Submarine work environments with constant artificial lighting contributed to cumulative neurological strain over 16 years. | Triggered by environment. Recovery: hours. |
| | Sensory shutdown — system overwhelm leading to functional collapse | When sensory input exceeds processing capacity, executive function ceases. Cannot speak, make decisions, or move purposefully. Not a choice. A neurological event. | 2-4 times per week. Duration: 30 min to several hours. |
| **COMMUNICATION** | Processing delay — gap between receiving speech and comprehending meaning | Appears to understand in real time (masking) but actually processing 2-5 seconds behind. Frequently agrees to things not fully understood. Misses implied meaning, sarcasm, subtext. | Every conversation. Compensated by scripting and rehearsal. |
| | Expressive language difficulty — discrepancy between internal understanding and verbal output | Can write complex technical documents but cannot explain the same content verbally under pressure. Phone calls require preparation. Spontaneous conversation causes anxiety and errors. | Triggered by unscripted interaction. |
| | Social reciprocity deficits — inability to read/respond to social cues automatically | Misreads facial expressions, tone of voice, and implied expectations. Over-discloses or under-responds. Relationships deteriorate without understanding why. 16 years of DoD service involved constant conscious social performance. | Every interaction. Masking cost: cumulative exhaustion. |
| **ENERGY MANAGEMENT** | Finite daily capacity — total functional energy is fixed and non-renewable within a day | One medical appointment, one phone call, or one social interaction can consume the entire day's functional capacity. Cannot sustain 8 hours of any activity requiring executive function, social performance, or sensory tolerance. | Daily. No recovery method other than sleep. |
| | Post-exertional cognitive depletion — disproportionate recovery from normal activities | Activities that are routine for neurotypical adults (grocery shopping, attending a meeting, cooking dinner) require 2-4x the recovery time. A 1-hour appointment may require 3-4 hours of low-stimulation recovery. | After every demanding activity. |
| | Hypoparathyroidism fatigue — chronic endocrine fatigue compounding neurodevelopmental limits | Low calcium episodes cause muscle cramps, brain fog, and fatigue independent of AuDHD. Combined effect: baseline energy is already reduced before executive function demands begin. Daily medication (calcitriol, calcium carbonate) manages but does not eliminate symptoms. | Daily. 22 years continuous. Residual symptoms despite treatment compliance. |
| **SOCIAL INTERACTION** | Masking exhaustion — decades of performing neurotypical behavior | 39 years of unconscious masking, 16 years in hierarchical DoD environment requiring strict communication protocols. Post-diagnosis, maintaining the mask became psychologically unsustainable. Authentic self-presentation is socially costly; masked presentation is energetically unsustainable. | Constant tension. No resolution except reduced social demands. |
| | Isolation and withdrawal — avoidance of interaction to preserve function | Declining social contact is not preference but necessity. Interaction depletes resources needed for basic self-care and parenting. Current social contact limited primarily to children (via Messenger Kids) and essential service providers. | Progressive since diagnosis. |
| **WORKPLACE FUNCTION** | Cannot sustain competitive employment — combination of above limitations | Can perform complex cognitive work (software engineering, electrical systems analysis) in hyperfocused bursts of 2-4 hours. Cannot: maintain 8-hour attendance, navigate workplace social dynamics, tolerate open office environments, attend meetings reliably, manage supervisor relationships without masking cost, respond to schedule changes, or sustain performance across consecutive days. | The gap between burst capacity and sustained capacity IS the disability. |
| | Work history demonstrates decline, not capability | GS-05 to GS-12 over 16 years reflects extreme compensatory effort, not ease of function. Performance required 2-3x the energy expenditure of neurotypical peers. Departure from DoD in May 2025 was the point at which compensation exceeded capacity. | 16 years of unsustainable performance → collapse. |
