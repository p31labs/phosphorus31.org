# 60-Second Verbal Pitch Script — P31 Labs
**For:** Accelerator interviews, demo days, elevator conversations  
**Target:** 150-170 words (natural speaking pace)  
**Date:** February 2026

---

## THE SCRIPT

**0-10 seconds — The Hook:**
Picture an autistic adult in a grocery store, mid-meltdown. They can't speak. Their $12,000 speech device needs Wi‑Fi that isn't there. It's a paperweight. This happens every day. Fifteen percent of people are neurodivergent. The tools we have don't work when we need them most.

**10-25 seconds — The Solution:**
We're building Node One: a $200 handheld device that works entirely offline. LoRa mesh networking means no internet, no cell towers—just devices talking to each other. Haptic feedback provides sensory regulation. It tracks medication interactions that single-purpose apps miss. And The Buffer—our communication layer—filters high-stress messages before they trigger overload. It's infrastructure, not inspiration.

**25-40 seconds — Why Us:**
I spent 16 years maintaining safety-critical electrical systems for the Department of Defense. Redundancy, fault tolerance, offline operation—that's my engineering discipline. I'm also AuDHD, managing hypoparathyroidism. I live the problem. When calcium supplements block my ADHD medication because I took them too close together, existing tools can't track that cascade. I'm building what I needed and couldn't find.

**40-50 seconds — Traction:**
The Scope—our cognitive dashboard—is in production. I use it daily. It tracks medication timing, energy levels, and prevents those biochemical conflicts. Node One firmware runs on ESP32-S3 hardware. The Buffer processes messages in real time. This isn't a concept. It's working code.

**50-60 seconds — The Ask:**
We need MATA's ecosystem: regulatory guidance for FDA Class II pathway, user research access to AuDHD adults testing in real-world scenarios, and institutional partnerships with schools and therapy practices. We have the engineering. We need the network. Help us scale what already works.

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

**Pacing:**
- Hook: 2.5 seconds per sentence (create tension, don't rush)
- Solution: 3 seconds per sentence (clear, confident)
- Why Us: 2.5 seconds per sentence (personal, authentic)
- Traction: 2 seconds per sentence (factual, punchy)
- The Ask: 2 seconds per sentence (direct, specific)

**Memorable Image:**
The $12,000 paperweight in the grocery store. This visual anchors the entire pitch—expensive tools that fail when infrastructure fails.

**Body Language:**
- Hook: Lean forward slightly (create engagement)
- Solution: Open gesture (show the device concept)
- Why Us: Hand to chest briefly (personal connection)
- Traction: Confident posture (proof of execution)
- The Ask: Open palms (collaborative invitation)

**Vocal Emphasis:**
- "Paperweight" (hook) — slight pause after
- "Infrastructure, not inspiration" (solution) — clear distinction
- "I live the problem" (why us) — authentic, not performative
- "Working code" (traction) — confident, factual
- "Help us scale" (ask) — collaborative, not demanding

## VARIATIONS

### For Technical Audiences
Replace "LoRa mesh networking" with "915MHz LoRa radio using Meshtastic protocol" if time allows.

### For Investor Audiences
Add after traction: "We're pre-revenue, bootstrapped, with fiscal sponsorship from Hack Club HCB."

### For Clinical Audiences
Emphasize after solution: "Class II assistive device pathway, not medical treatment—the regulatory boundary matters."

## PRACTICE PROMPTS

1. Record yourself delivering this script. Listen for:
   - Natural pauses (not rushed)
   - Authentic tone (not salesy)
   - Clear articulation (every word matters)

2. Practice in front of a mirror. Watch for:
   - Eye contact (even if speaking to camera/phone)
   - Natural gestures (not scripted)
   - Confident posture (shoulders back, not defensive)

3. Time yourself. Adjust pacing if you consistently run over 65 seconds or under 55 seconds.
