# Market Analysis and Impact Projection: P31 Labs

## Market Opportunity

The global assistive technology market reached $26 billion in 2024, with projections to $37 billion by 2030. Within this, the augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) device segment represents approximately $3.5 billion. However, these figures mask a critical gap: 15–20% of the global population is neurodivergent, and 1 in 36 children are diagnosed with autism (CDC ADDM 2023). AuDHD co-occurrence is increasingly recognized but remains underserved by existing solutions.

Current market leaders serve clinical settings with devices priced at $3,000–$15,000, requiring insurance pre-authorization and proprietary ecosystems. Consumer alternatives depend on cloud services and data monetization, failing during offline episodes when communication needs are most acute. The missing middle—affordable, open-source, privacy-respecting hardware—represents an underserved segment that existing market sizing does not capture.

## Competitive Landscape

**Tobii Dynavox** dominates clinical AAC with $5,000–$15,000 devices, proprietary software, and institutional sales channels. **Proloquo** offers iPad-based solutions at $250 plus hardware, but cloud dependency creates failure points during network outages. The **Meshtastic** project provides open-source LoRa mesh networking but lacks assistive technology focus or accessibility features. Various sensory regulation tools exist in isolation, without communication integration or medication management.

P31 Labs' differentiation: the only open-source hardware-plus-software assistive technology ecosystem combining mesh networking, haptic regulation, and cognitive dashboard integration. Built by someone who uses it, addressing multi-condition complexity (AuDHD plus hypoparathyroidism) that single-purpose tools cannot track.

## Impact Projection

**Year 1:** 50–100 beta devices deployed through Georgia Tech Tools for Life network, user research with AuDHD adults, validation of haptic-first interaction patterns and medication interaction tracking.

**Year 2:** Open hardware design published via Zenodo with defensive publication DOIs, community builds begin, 500+ devices in circulation through institutional partnerships and direct-to-user kit sales at cost ($45–65 BOM).

**Year 3:** Class II 510(k) submission for FDA clearance, expanded institutional partnerships, 2,000+ devices deployed. Indirect impact: defensive publications establish prior art, preventing patent trolls from locking up mesh-based assistive technology and haptic-regulation techniques.

## Business Model

P31 Labs operates as a Georgia 501(c)(3) nonprofit (filing in progress), with interim fiscal sponsorship through Hack Club HCB. Revenue streams: grants (SBIR/STTR, foundation), device kit sales at cost, and training/consulting for institutions. Not pursuing SaaS subscriptions, data monetization, or locked ecosystems. Open-source licensing (Apache 2.0) ensures freedom to operate and community contribution, reducing barriers to adoption while maintaining sustainability through grant funding and cost-recovery kit sales.

The market opportunity is not measured in device sales alone, but in preventing preventable medical crises, reducing caregiver burnout, and establishing open infrastructure that others can build upon. At $45–65 BOM versus $3,000–$15,000 clinical alternatives, the cost differential enables deployment at scale that proprietary solutions cannot match.
