## 1. GRIEVANCE SUMMARY

### Nature of Grievance: ABANDONMENT & UNAUTHORIZED PRACTICE

Attorney Joseph East engaged in systematic professional misconduct including:

1. **Client Abandonment** - Failed to protect client interests upon termination
2. **Unauthorized Practice** - Allowed signature to be used post-termination
3. **Professional Negligence** - Failed to diligently secure client's docket
4. **Financial Harm** - Caused catastrophic financial loss to client

### Financial Impact: $77,000+ in Direct Losses

The misconduct resulted in the illegal seizure of client's assets and catastrophic financial harm through forced liquidation of retirement accounts.

## 2. PROFESSIONAL MISCONDUCT ALLEGATIONS

### 2.1 Client Abandonment (Primary Violation)

**Incident Date:** October 19-23, 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney East abandoned his client during critical legal proceedings by:

1. **Failing to Protect Client Interests:** Did not secure client's docket before termination
2. **Failing to Monitor Proceedings:** Allowed opposing counsel to seize assets
3. **Failing to Withdraw Consent:** Did not withdraw consent to any pending matters
4. **Failing to Protect Client:** Left client vulnerable to legal attack

**Evidence:**
- **October 19, 2025:** Client terminated East for cause
- **October 20, 2025:** Court signed Order Permitting Withdrawal
- **October 23, 2025:** Opposing counsel seized client's assets using East's signature

**Legal Violation:** Abandonment under Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct Rule 1.16

### 2.2 Unauthorized Practice of Law

**Incident Date:** October 23, 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney East engaged in unauthorized practice by allowing his signature to be used on court documents after his formal withdrawal from the case.

**Legal Violation:** Unauthorized practice under Rule 5.5

**Criminal Implications:** Potential criminal charges for practicing law without authority

### 2.3 Professional Negligence

**Incident Date:** October 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney East failed to provide competent representation by:

1. **Failing to Protect Client Assets:** Did not secure client's financial interests
2. **Failing to Monitor Case:** Allowed case to deteriorate
3. **Failing to Act Diligently:** Did not take necessary protective actions
4. **Failing to Communicate:** Did not warn client of potential risks

**Financial Impact:** $77,000+ in direct financial losses

- Failed to take necessary protective actions

- Failed to pursue client's interests
- Failed to take timely action

- Failed to keep client reasonably informed
- Failed to explain matters to client
- Failed to respond to client's concerns

**Rule 1.16: Declining or Terminating Representation**
- Failed to protect client's interests upon termination
- Failed to take steps to avoid foreseeable prejudice
- Failed to surrender papers and property to client

**Rule 1.17: Sale of Law Practice**
- Failed to properly withdraw from representation
- Failed to protect client's interests during transition
