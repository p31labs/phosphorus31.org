### 1. Permanent Hypoparathyroidism (ICD-10: E20.9)
**Diagnosis Date:** [REDACTED]  
**Treating Physician:** [REDACTED]  
**Status:** Permanent, Life-long Condition  

**Clinical Description:**
Hypoparathyroidism is a rare endocrine disorder characterized by insufficient production of parathyroid hormone (PTH), leading to chronically low calcium levels (hypocalcemia). This condition requires lifelong medical management and can be life-threatening if not properly controlled.

**Symptoms:**
- Muscle cramps and spasms
- Tingling or numbness in extremities
- Seizures
- Cardiac arrhythmias
- Cognitive impairment
- Anxiety and depression

**Treatment Requirements:**
- Daily calcium supplementation
- Vitamin D analogs (calcitriol)
- Regular blood monitoring
- Emergency protocols for severe hypocalcemia
