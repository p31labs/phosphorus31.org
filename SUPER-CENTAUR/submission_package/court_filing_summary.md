## MATHEMATICAL IMPOSSIBILITY ANALYSIS

### The Core Discrepancy:

**Claimed Financial Situation:**
- Annual Income: $38,400 ($3,200 × 12)
- Annual Expenses: $49,200 ($4,100 × 12)  
- **Annual Deficit: $10,800**

**Actual Financial Activity (2-month sample):**
- Net Income: $9,411.00
- Annualized Income: $56,466
- **Positive Cash Flow: $56,466**

### Mathematical Proof of Impossibility:

1. **Income Discrepancy:** Claimed income is 32% lower than actual documented income
2. **Expense Manipulation:** Claimed expenses appear inflated or include non-essential items
3. **Undisclosed Support:** $2,670.71 in unreported financial support from father
4. **Litigation Funding:** Ability to fund legal proceedings while claiming inability to pay basic expenses

**Conclusion:** It is mathematically impossible to have a $10,800 annual deficit while maintaining positive account balances, funding litigation, and supporting a discretionary lifestyle.
