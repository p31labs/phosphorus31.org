# GEORGIA STATE BAR COMPLAINT AND REQUEST FOR FEE ARBITRATION

**RESPONDENT ATTORNEY:** J. Alan Welch (Bar No. 746765)

**GRIEVANT:** William R. Johnson

## I. SUMMARY OF GRIEVANCE: ABANDONMENT AND BREACH OF FIDUCIARY DUTY

This grievance is filed against J. Alan Welch, who accepted a retainer to represent the Grievant, a disabled veteran with permanent Hypoparathyroidism. The core allegations are:

1. **Failure to Act on Exculpatory Evidence**: Mr. Welch received irrefutable evidence of financial fraud by the opposing party but failed to file or act upon it.

2. **Coercion and Detrimental Advice**: Mr. Welch attempted to coerce the Grievant into signing a consent order drafted by opposing counsel that would have been detrimental to the Grievant's interests.

3. **Abandonment During Medical Crisis**: Mr. Welch withdrew from representation during a documented, life-threatening metabolic crisis of the Grievant, despite possessing specialized medical knowledge as a Registered Pharmacist.

4. **Improper Retention of Unearned Fees**: Upon withdrawal, Mr. Welch refunded only 50% of the unearned retainer fees, leaving the Grievant in a worse legal position.

## II. DETAILED STATEMENT OF FACTS

### A. Possession and Failure to Use Exculpatory Evidence

1. **October 31, 2025**: The Grievant provided Mr. Welch with a forensic financial analysis ("Joint Checking Activity Report") that proved the Plaintiff had manufactured the mortgage delinquency by withholding income (a $320 contribution versus a $3,105 withdrawal).

2. **Failure to Act**: Despite possessing this "smoking gun" evidence that would have defeated the Plaintiff's "Emergency Motion," Mr. Welch refused to file it with the court or use it in any meaningful way to protect the Grievant's interests.

### B. Attempted Coercion and Ineffective Assistance

**October 30, 2025**: Mr. Welch forwarded a "2nd Consent Order" drafted by opposing counsel, urging the Grievant to sign. Acceptance of this order would have validated the fraudulent seizure of the Grievant's assets and permanently damaged his legal position.

### C. Abandonment During a Documented Medical Crisis

1. **January 2026**: The Grievant informed Mr. Welch that he was experiencing a "Medical Blackout" (Calcium 7.8 mg/dL), a state of medical incapacity brought on by the stress of the litigation and lack of funds for medication.

2. **Dual Licensure Knowledge**: Mr. Welch, holding dual licensure as an Attorney and a Registered Pharmacist, possesses specialized medical knowledge regarding the lethality of Hypocalcemia (low calcium).

3. **Abandonment**: Knowing the Grievant was medically incapacitated and facing imminent homelessness, Mr. Welch withdrew from the case rather than filing for an emergency stay, thereby violating the higher standard of care expected of a dual-license holder.

### D. Improper Retention of Fees

Upon withdrawal, Mr. Welch refunded an arbitrary amount: exactly half of the retainer. This partial refund is viewed as an admission that services were not rendered; however, Mr. Welch retained the other half despite abandoning the case at a critical juncture and leaving the Grievant in a more precarious legal situation.

## III. VIOLATIONS OF GEORGIA RULES OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

### Rule 1.1 - Competence

Mr. Welch violated Rule 1.1 by failing to provide competent representation, including the failure to act on exculpatory evidence and the failure to protect the client's interests during a medical crisis.

### Rule 1.3 - Diligence

Mr. Welch violated Rule 1.3 by failing to act with reasonable diligence and promptness in representing the client, including the abandonment during a critical period.

### Rule 1.4 - Communication

Mr. Welch violated Rule 1.4 by failing to keep the client reasonably informed about the status of the matter and by failing to explain matters to the extent reasonably necessary to permit the client to make informed decisions.

### Rule 1.7 - Conflict of Interest

Mr. Welch violated Rule 1.7 by allowing his own interests (avoiding a difficult case) to conflict with the client's interests.

### Rule 1.16 - Declining or Terminating Representation

Mr. Welch violated Rule 1.16 by failing to take reasonable steps to avoid foreseeable prejudice to the rights of the client upon termination of representation.

### Rule 8.4 - Misconduct

Mr. Welch violated Rule 8.4 by engaging in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation, including the improper retention of fees.

## IV. REQUEST FOR FEE ARBITRATION

1. The Grievant formally requests a review and arbitration of the retained portion of the retainer fee, given the premature and detrimental withdrawal from the case and the retention of fees for services that were not rendered or which resulted in a negative outcome for the client.

2. The services were not rendered, yet Mr. Welch retained the other half despite leaving the case in a worse position than he found it.

## V. RELIEF REQUESTED

The Grievant requests that the State Bar of Georgia:

1. **Investigate** Mr. Welch's conduct for violations of the Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct.

2. **Order** full restitution of all unearned fees.

3. **Pursue** appropriate disciplinary action for the professional misconduct outlined above.

4. **Refer** the fee dispute to the appropriate fee arbitration committee.
