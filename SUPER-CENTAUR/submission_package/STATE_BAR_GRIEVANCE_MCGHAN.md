# STATE BAR GRIEVANCE

**TO:** State Bar of Georgia, Office of the General Counsel

**RE:** Grievance Against Jennifer L. McGhan (Bar No. 649444)

**GRIEVANT:** William R. Johnson

## I. NATURE OF GRIEVANCE: FRAUD UPON THE COURT & MISREPRESENTATION

Attorney McGhan engaged in professional misconduct by:

1. **Filing Fabricated Orders**: Knowingly filing court orders bearing the signature of an attorney she knew had been terminated and withdrawn, thereby effectively fabricating consent to seize the Grievant's assets.

2. **Material Misrepresentation of Law**: Making material misrepresentations of federal tax law to the Court to force a financial liquidation of a qualified retirement account.

## II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

### A. The "Zombie Signature" Fraud

**Knowledge of Withdrawal**: On October 20, 2025, the Superior Court entered an Order Permitting Withdrawal for the Grievant's former counsel, Joseph East. Attorney McGhan received this order. On October 21, the Grievant served Attorney McGhan with a Notification of No Counsel, confirming his pro se status.

**The Unauthorized Filing**: On October 23, 2025, knowing Mr. East had no authority to act, Attorney McGhan filed a "Consent Temporary Order" and an "Emergency Order" bearing Mr. East's signature.

**Intent and Violation**: Attorney McGhan utilized the signature of the terminated lawyer to bypass the Grievant's pro se objections and secure the seizure of assets without a hearing. This conduct violates Rule 3.3 (Candor to the Tribunal) and Rule 8.4 (Misconduct) of the Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct.

### B. The Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) Tax Misrepresentation

**The Misrepresentation**: Attorney McGhan represented to the Court and the Grievant that a 10% early withdrawal penalty on the Grievant's Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) was "unavoidable."

**The Truth**: Title 26 U.S.C. § 72(t)(2)(C) explicitly exempts transfers of retirement funds to a spouse or former spouse pursuant to a divorce decree or court order from this 10% early withdrawal penalty.

**Damage Caused**: This material misrepresentation of federal tax law caused an unnecessary and avoidable financial loss of $7,079.19 to the marital estate, which the Grievant was forced to incur as part of the liquidation.

## III. VIOLATIONS OF GEORGIA RULES OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

### Rule 3.3 - Candor Toward the Tribunal

Attorney McGhan violated Rule 3.3(a)(1) by knowingly making a false statement of fact or law to the tribunal and failing to correct a false statement of material fact or law previously made to the tribunal.

### Rule 8.4 - Misconduct

Attorney McGhan violated Rule 8.4(c) by engaging in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation, and Rule 8.4(d) by engaging in conduct that is prejudicial to the administration of justice.

## IV. RELIEF REQUESTED

The Grievant requests a full and immediate investigation into Attorney Jennifer L. McGhan's conduct for:

1. **Manufacturing and filing fraudulent "Consent" orders with the Court.**
2. **Misleading the tribunal regarding explicit provisions of federal tax statutes.**
