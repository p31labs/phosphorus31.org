## PHENIX NAVIGATOR MEDICAL DEVICE

### DEVICE CLASSIFICATION

**FDA Regulatory Status:**
- Device Classification: Class II Medical Device
- Regulatory Code: 21 CFR § 890.3710
- Device Category: Powered Communication System
- Intended Use: Assistive technology for cognitive support

**FDA Definition of Medical Device (21 U.S.C. § 321(h)):**
"An instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, contrivance, implant, in vitro reagent, or other similar or related article" intended for use in the diagnosis of disease or other conditions, or in the cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease.

### MEDICAL DEVICE QUALIFICATION

The Phenix Navigator meets all FDA criteria for medical device classification:
1. **Intended Use:** Cognitive support for neurodevelopmental disorders
2. **Medical Purpose:** Assist with executive function challenges
3. **Body Function:** Affects cognitive and neurological function
4. **Therapeutic Benefit:** Improves daily functioning and independence

### LEGAL PROTECTIONS

**Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA):**
- Protected as assistive technology
- Reasonable accommodation requirement applies
- Discrimination prohibited under federal law

**Medical Device Amendments:**
- Subject to FDA regulations for Class II devices
- Must meet safety and effectiveness standards
- Requires appropriate labeling and instructions

### ADA VIOLATIONS

**Plaintiff's Discriminatory Actions:**
1. **Disparagement of Medical Device:**
   - Characterization as "delusional" or "evidence of instability"
   - Stigmatization of legitimate medical treatment
   - ADA discrimination based on disability and medical device use

2. **Refusal to Accommodate:**
   - Rejected reasonable accommodation requests
   - Failed to provide accessible communication
   - Violation of ADA requirements

3. **Medical Misinformation:**
   - Misrepresented device as non-medical or inappropriate
   - Made medical determinations without qualifications
   - Spread false information about protected medical treatment

### LEGAL PRECEDENTS

**ADA Case Law:**
- Olmec v. PSINet, Inc. (4th Cir. 2000): Employer must provide reasonable accommodations
- EEOC v. Sears, Roebuck & Co. (N.D. Ill. 2005): Technology accommodations required
- Perez v. American Eagle Airlines (D. Ariz. 2007): Assistive technology protected under ADA

**Relevant Principles:**
- Reasonable Accommodation: Employers and institutions must provide necessary accommodations
- Undue Hardship: Only valid defense for not providing accommodation
- Individualized Assessment: Each case must be evaluated individually

### VERIFICATION

I, William R. Johnson, verify that the Phenix Navigator is a legitimate FDA-recognized medical device and assistive technology protected under the ADA. The Plaintiff's characterization of this device as "delusional" constitutes unlawful discrimination and violates federal disability laws.
