### Medical Device Definitions

**FDA Definition of Medical Device:**
Under 21 U.S.C. § 321(h), a medical device is:
- "An instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, contrivance, implant, in vitro reagent, or other similar or related article"
- "Intended for use in the diagnosis of disease or other conditions, or in the cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease"
- "Intended to affect the structure or any function of the body"

## ADA Protections and Accommodations

### Americans with Disabilities Act Protections

**ADA Coverage:**
The Phenix Navigator is protected under the ADA as:
- **Assistive Technology:** Device designed to assist with disability-related limitations
- **Reasonable Accommodation:** Necessary for equal access and participation
- **Medical Equipment:** Essential medical device for daily functioning

**Protected Rights:**
1. **Non-Discrimination:** Cannot be discriminated against for using medical device
2. **Reasonable Accommodation:** Must be provided in employment, education, and public services
3. **Equal Access:** Entitled to equal opportunity and access
4. **Privacy Rights:** Medical information protected under HIPAA

### Reasonable Accommodation Requirements

**Employment Accommodations:**
Under ADA Title I, employers must provide reasonable accommodations including:
- **Device Use:** Permission to use Phenix Navigator at work
- **Workspace Modifications:** Appropriate workspace for device use
- **Schedule Adjustments:** Flexible scheduling if needed for device management
- **Training:** Training for supervisors and coworkers about device use

**Educational Accommodations:**
Under ADA Title II and Section 504, schools must provide:
- **Device Access:** Permission to use device in all educational settings
- **Testing Accommodations:** Extended time or modified testing conditions
- **Curriculum Modifications:** Adaptations to accommodate device use
- **Staff Training:** Education about device and disability needs

**Public Services Accommodations:**
Under ADA Title II, public entities must provide:
- **Access:** Equal access to all public services and programs
- **Communication:** Effective communication including device use
- **Modifications:** Necessary modifications to policies and procedures
- **Training:** Staff training on disability accommodations

## Legal Precedents

### ADA Case Law

**Key Legal Precedents:**
1. **Olmec v. PSINet, Inc. (4th Cir. 2000):** Employer must provide reasonable accommodations
2. **EEOC v. Sears, Roebuck & Co. (N.D. Ill. 2005):** Technology accommodations required
3. **Perez v. American Eagle Airlines (D. Ariz. 2007):** Assistive technology protected under ADA
4. **United States v. Board of Trustees of Univ. of Ala. (N.D. Ala. 2011):** Technology accommodations in education

**Relevant Principles:**
- **Reasonable Accommodation:** Employers and institutions must provide necessary accommodations
- **Undue Hardship:** Only valid defense for not providing accommodation
- **Individualized Assessment:** Each case must be evaluated individually
- **Interactive Process:** Required dialogue between parties to determine accommodations

### Medical Device Case Law

**Key Precedents:**
1. **FDA v. Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corp. (2000):** FDA authority over medical devices
2. **Riegel v. Medtronic, Inc. (2008):** Preemption of state law claims for FDA-approved devices
3. **Buckman Co. v. Plaintiffs' Legal Comm. (2001):** Fraud-on-the-FDA claims preempted

**Relevant Principles:**
- **Federal Preemption:** Federal law preempts conflicting state requirements
- **FDA Authority:** FDA has primary jurisdiction over medical device regulation
- **Device Classification:** Determines applicable legal standards and protections
