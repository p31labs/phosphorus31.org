## 2. PROFESSIONAL MISCONDUCT ALLEGATIONS

### 2.1 Fraud Upon the Court (Primary Violation)

**Incident Date:** October 23, 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney McGhan knowingly filed "Consent Temporary Order" and "Emergency Temporary Order" bearing the signature of Joseph East, an attorney she knew had been terminated and withdrawn from the case.

**Evidence:**
- **October 19, 2025:** Defendant terminated Joseph East for cause
- **October 20, 2025:** Court signed Order Permitting Withdrawal of Counsel
- **October 21, 2025:** Defendant served Notice of No Counsel on McGhan
- **October 23, 2025:** McGhan filed orders with East's signature

**Legal Violation:** Fraud upon the court under Lewis v. Uselton, 202 Ga. App. 875 (1992)

### 2.2 Tax Law Misrepresentation

**Incident Date:** October 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney McGhan misrepresented federal tax law to the Court and client, claiming the 10% early withdrawal penalty on TSP was "unavoidable."

**Actual Law:** 26 U.S.C. § 72(t)(2)(C) provides clear exception for distributions to spouses under divorce decrees.

**Financial Impact:** $7,079.19 in avoidable penalties

**Legal Violation:** Professional negligence and misrepresentation of law

### 2.3 ADA Discrimination

**Incident Date:** October 2025

**Allegation:**
Attorney McGhan discriminated against client with disabilities by:

1. **Refusing Accommodation:** Rejected reasonable ADA accommodation request
2. **Retaliation:** Punished client for requesting accommodations
3. **Disparagement:** Characterized medical device as "delusional"

**Disabilities:** Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD, Permanent Hypoparathyroidism

**Legal Violation:** ADA discrimination and retaliation

## 5. LEGAL AUTHORITY

### 5.1 Fraud Upon the Court

**Lewis v. Uselton, 202 Ga. App. 875 (1992):**
- Void orders based on consent of terminated attorney
- Requires immediate vacation of fraudulent judgments

**O.C.G.A. § 9-11-60(d):**
- Provides 3-year window for fraud claims
- Allows vacation of void judgments

### 5.2 Professional Discipline

**In the Matter of Hines, 271 Ga. 80 (1999):**
- Standard for attorney discipline
- Factors for determining appropriate sanction

**In the Matter of Smith, 283 Ga. 510 (2008):**
- Fraud upon the court warrants severe discipline
- Pattern of misconduct increases severity

### 5.3 ADA Violations

**42 U.S.C. § 12132:**
- Prohibits discrimination in public services
- Applies to court proceedings

**28 C.F.R. Part 35:**
- Implements ADA requirements
- Mandates reasonable accommodations
