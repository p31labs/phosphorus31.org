## Legal Violations

### Violation 1: Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

**Legal Standard:**
Title II of the ADA requires public entities to provide reasonable accommodations to individuals with disabilities unless it would cause an undue burden.

**Violation:**
- **Failure to Provide Accommodations:** Categorical refusal without consideration
- **Failure to Engage in Interactive Process:** No discussion or negotiation of accommodations
- **Discriminatory Treatment:** Treating disabled individual differently without justification

**Legal Citation:**
42 U.S.C. § 12131-12134 (Title II of ADA)

### Violation 2: Rehabilitation Act of 1973

**Legal Standard:**
Section 504 prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in programs receiving federal financial assistance.

**Violation:**
- **Discrimination Based on Disability:** Refusing accommodations available to non-disabled individuals
- **Failure to Provide Equal Access:** Denying meaningful participation in legal proceedings

**Legal Citation:**
29 U.S.C. § 794 (Section 504 of Rehabilitation Act)

### Violation 3: Due Process Rights

**Legal Standard:**
Fourteenth Amendment guarantees due process, including meaningful access to legal proceedings.

**Violation:**
- **Procedural Due Process:** Denying ability to fully participate in proceedings
- **Substantive Due Process:** Arbitrary and capricious treatment based on disability

**Legal Citation:**
U.S. Const. amend. XIV

### Violation 4: Georgia Disability Discrimination Law

**Legal Standard:**
Georgia law prohibits disability discrimination and requires reasonable accommodations.

**Violation:**
- **State Law Violation:** Failure to comply with Georgia accommodation requirements
- **Discriminatory Intent:** Explicit refusal to accommodate known disabilities

**Legal Citation:**
O.C.G.A. § 31-1-6
