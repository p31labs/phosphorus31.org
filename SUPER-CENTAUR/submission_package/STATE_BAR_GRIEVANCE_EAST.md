# STATE BAR GRIEVANCE AGAINST JOSEPH EAST

**TO:** State Bar of Georgia

**RE:** Grievance Against Joseph East (Bar No. 237560)

**GRIEVANT:** William R. Johnson

## I. NATURE OF GRIEVANCE: UNAUTHORIZED PRACTICE & ABANDONMENT

Mr. East allowed his signature to be used on a "Consent Order" that was entered three days after he was formally removed from the case by Judicial Order. This action resulted in the illegal seizure of his former client's assets.

## II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

### Termination of Counsel

I terminated Mr. East for cause on October 19, 2025, via email. The termination was based on material breaches of the Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct, including failure to provide competent representation and failure to act with reasonable diligence.

### Judicial Withdrawal

Judge O. Brent Green signed the Order Permitting Withdrawal on October 20, 2025, formally removing Mr. East as counsel. This order was entered into the court record and became effective immediately.

### The Violation

On October 23, 2025, a "Consent Order" was filed bearing Mr. East's signature. This filing occurred three days after his formal removal from the case.

### Analysis of Professional Misconduct

This filing presents two scenarios, both constituting professional misconduct:

**Scenario A: Unauthorized Practice and Fraud (Signature after Withdrawal)**
If Mr. East signed this "Consent Order" after October 20, 2025, he committed the Unauthorized Practice of Law and Fraud, as he was no longer counsel of record and had no authority to act on behalf of the client.

**Scenario B: Malpractice (Failure to Withdraw Consent)**
If he signed it before his termination, he committed Malpractice by failing to withdraw his consent upon being terminated, with the knowledge that the order would be used to the detriment and catastrophic financial harm of his former client.

### Financial Harm

Mr. East refunded only 50% of the retainer, acknowledging that services were incomplete. His failure to diligently secure the docket caused catastrophic financial harm to the Grievant, resulting in a loss of $77,000.

## III. VIOLATIONS OF GEORGIA RULES OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

### Rule 1.16 - Declining or Terminating Representation

Mr. East violated Rule 1.16 by failing to take reasonable steps to avoid foreseeable prejudice to the rights of the client upon termination of representation.

### Rule 1.1 - Competence

Mr. East violated Rule 1.1 by failing to provide competent representation to the client, including the failure to properly withdraw consent and protect the client's interests.

### Rule 1.3 - Diligence

Mr. East violated Rule 1.3 by failing to act with reasonable diligence and promptness in representing the client.

### Rule 8.4 - Misconduct

Mr. East violated Rule 8.4 by engaging in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation.

## IV. RELIEF REQUESTED

I request that the State Bar of Georgia pursue disciplinary action against Joseph East for:

1. **Failing to protect a client's interests upon termination** (Violation of Rule 1.16 of the Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct).

2. **Allowing a "Zombie Signature" to bind a former client**, resulting in the illegal seizure of assets.
