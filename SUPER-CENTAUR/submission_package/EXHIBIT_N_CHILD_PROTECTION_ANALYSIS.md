## Legal Standards and Requirements

### Georgia Child Protection Laws

**O.C.G.A. § 19-7-1 et seq.:**
Georgia's child protection laws establish standards for child custody and protection.

**Key Provisions:**
1. **Best Interests Standard:** Primary consideration in custody decisions
2. **Child Safety:** Paramount concern in all custody determinations
3. **Parental Fitness:** Must be evaluated based on ability to provide safe environment
4. **Financial Responsibility:** Parent's financial stability is relevant factor

### Federal Child Protection Standards

**CAPTA (Child Abuse Prevention and Treatment Act):**
Federal standards for child protection and welfare.

**Key Provisions:**
1. **Child Safety:** Primary consideration in all decisions
2. **Family Preservation:** Preference for keeping families together when safe
3. **Risk Assessment:** Systematic assessment of risks and protective factors
4. **Services Provision:** Requirement to provide appropriate support services

### Professional Standards

**American Academy of Pediatrics:**
Standards for child custody and visitation arrangements.

**Key Principles:**
1. **Child-Centered:** Decisions must prioritize children's needs
2. **Evidence-Based:** Decisions must be based on objective evidence
3. **Developmental Appropriateness:** Arrangements must consider children's developmental needs
4. **Safety Paramount:** Child safety is the primary consideration
