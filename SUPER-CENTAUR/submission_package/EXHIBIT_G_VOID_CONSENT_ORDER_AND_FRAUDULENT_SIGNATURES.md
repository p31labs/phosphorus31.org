## Fraud Upon the Court

### Elements of Fraud

**1. False Representation:**
- Joseph East's signature implied continued representation
- False representation of authority to act

**2. Knowledge of Falsity:**
- Opposing counsel knew of termination (October 21)
- Knew Joseph East had no authority
- Intentional deception of the court

**3. Intent to Deceive:**
- Purposeful use of terminated attorney's signature
- Bypass Defendant's pro se objections
- Obtain orders without proper notice

**4. Justifiable Reliance:**
- Court relied on apparent consent
- No opportunity for Defendant to contest
- Fraudulent appearance of agreement

**5. Damages:**
- Illegal seizure of assets
- Financial harm ($70,791.85)
- Violation of due process rights

### Legal Precedent

**Lewis v. Uselton, 202 Ga. App. 875 (1992):**
> "A judgment based on the consent of an attorney who lacks authority is void."

**Application:**
- Joseph East lacked all authority
- Orders based on fraudulent consent
- Orders are VOID AB INITIO
