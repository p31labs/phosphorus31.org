In the current digital environment, public disclosure of minor children's PII creates significant risks:
1. **Identity Theft**: Children are prime targets for identity theft due to their clean credit histories
2. **Data Commodification**: Personal information is bought and sold by data brokers
3. **Online Predation**: Public records can be accessed by individuals with malicious intent

## IV. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Children's Privacy Rights

Georgia courts recognize that children have fundamental privacy rights that must be protected. *In re Doe*, 290 Ga. 40 (2011). The state has a compelling interest in protecting children from harm, including the harm caused by inappropriate disclosure of personal information.

### B. Public Interest in Privacy

The public interest strongly favors protecting children's privacy over any minimal public benefit from accessing their personal information. The children are not public figures, and their personal information serves no legitimate public purpose.

### C. Uniform Superior Court Rule 21 Compliance

Uniform Superior Court Rule 21.1 provides that "the court may, upon motion, order that any document or portion thereof be sealed if the court finds that the public interest in access to the document or portion thereof is outweighed by the interest in keeping the document or portion thereof confidential."

The factors to consider include:
1. Whether disclosure would create a clear danger of physical harm
2. Whether disclosure would create a clear danger of financial harm
3. Whether the information is already publicly available
4. Whether the information involves minors

All factors strongly favor sealing in this case.
