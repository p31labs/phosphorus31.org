### COMPREHENSIVE HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS AND FUNCTIONAL IMPAIRMENT

Mr. William R. Johnson is a 39-year-old male with a psychiatric history notable for these DSM-5-Tr conditions:

**DIAGNOSES:**
- Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder, Combined Presentation (F90.0)
- Autism Spectrum Disorder (F84.0)
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (F41.1)
- Major Depressive Disorder, Recurrent, Moderate (F33.1)
- Insomnia Disorder (G47.00)

**OCCUPATIONAL BACKGROUND:**
Until May 2025, the patient was employed as an Engineering Technician with the Department of Defense, where he worked on highly sensitive and cognitively demanding tasks involving motors and generators for submarines. His position required sustained concentration, high executive function, collaboration with peers, and strict adherence to safety and procedural protocols.

**SYMPTOM ONSET AND PROGRESSION:**
The patient has struggled for many years with attention, organization, impulse control, and social communication, issues now understood to stem from his dual diagnoses of ADHD and Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), formally diagnosed in 2025. These core impairments led to significant functional decline, despite the patient's intellect and technical knowledge.

He also suffers from chronic anxiety and episodic depressive symptoms, worsened by stressors including occupational burnout, marital conflict, and custodial limitations following child protective services involvement. His anxiety has at times manifested as daily panic attacks lasting over an hour, which have rendered him incapacitated for both family and work obligations.

**FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS:**
- **Executive Function Impairments:** Rated "Highly Elevated" across all domains including working memory, organization, planning, and task initiation
- **Communication Differences:** Difficulty with spontaneous verbal communication in stressful situations, challenges understanding implied or non-literal communication
- **Processing Time Requirements:** Need for additional time to process complex legal information, difficulty making important decisions under arbitrary time pressure
- **Sensory Sensitivities:** Difficulty in busy, chaotic environments with multiple stimuli, heightened sensitivity to noise and visual distractions

**CURRENT TREATMENT:**
The patient is under active care and compliant with all prescribed treatment. He is currently applying for Federal Disability Retirement based on his inability to perform his previous duties as an Engineering Technician for the U.S. Department of Defense.

**PROGNOSIS:**
With continued treatment and appropriate accommodations, the patient shows gradual improvement in symptom management. However, without proper accommodations and support systems, his condition would significantly deteriorate, impacting his ability to function independently and maintain employment.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY OF PHENIX NAVIGATOR:**
The Phenix Navigator system is medically necessary for Mr. Johnson's treatment as it provides essential cognitive support, compensates for executive function deficits, and enables him to maintain daily functioning and independence.
