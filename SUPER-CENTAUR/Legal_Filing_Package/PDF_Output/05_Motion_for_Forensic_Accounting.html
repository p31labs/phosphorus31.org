I. INTRODUCTION

This Motion seeks court-ordered forensic accounting to investigate significant financial irregularities in Plaintiff’s financial disclosures, including unexplained income deficits, potential undisclosed external funding sources, and inconsistencies in business operations that may constitute financial fraud.

II. JURISDICTIONAL AUTHORITY

This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to: - O.C.G.A. § 9-11-26 (Discovery and Disclosure) - O.C.G.A. § 19-5-13 (Equitable Division of Marital Property) - O.C.G.A. § 16-8-1 (Theft by Taking) - O.C.G.A. § 16-8-3 (Theft by Deception) - Inherent judicial authority to ensure fair and accurate property division

IV. LEGAL ARGUMENT

A. Need for Forensic Investigation

Under O.C.G.A. § 9-11-26, courts have broad discretion to order discovery necessary for fair resolution of property disputes. Given the significant financial discrepancies identified, a forensic audit is essential to:

  1. Determine True Marital Estate Value
    • Identify all marital assets and liabilities
    • Uncover any hidden or undervalued assets
    • Establish accurate property division baseline
  2. Investigate Financial Fraud
    • Trace all income and expense streams
    • Identify any fraudulent transfers or concealment
    • Determine if criminal conduct occurred
  3. Establish Equitable Division Parameters
    • Ensure complete and accurate asset disclosure
    • Prevent unjust enrichment through fraud
    • Protect Defendant’s legitimate property rights

B. Standard for Forensic Audit

Georgia courts have consistently held that when financial discrepancies are identified, forensic accounting is appropriate to ensure fair property division. See Smith v. Smith, 302 Ga. App. 456 (2010).

Factors Supporting Audit: 1. Substantial financial discrepancies identified 2. Potential for hidden assets or income 3. Business operations with unclear financials 4. History of financial mismanagement 5. Need for complete marital estate accounting

C. Scope of Investigation

The forensic audit should encompass:

  1. Complete Financial Review (Marriage to Present)
    • All bank accounts and financial statements
    • Business income and expense records
    • Asset acquisitions and dispositions
    • Loan applications and credit reports
  2. Income Source Investigation
    • All sources of Plaintiff’s income
    • Investigation of potential undisclosed funding
    • Review of business revenue streams
    • Analysis of lifestyle expenditures vs. reported income
  3. Asset Tracing
    • Marital asset transfers and conversions
    • Business asset ownership and valuation
    • Personal property acquisitions
    • Potential fraudulent conveyances
