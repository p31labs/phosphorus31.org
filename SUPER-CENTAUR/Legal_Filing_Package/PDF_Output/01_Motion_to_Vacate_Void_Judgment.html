I. JURISDICTIONAL GROUNDS

This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 9-11-60(d)(2), which provides that a judgment is void when “the court rendering judgment had no jurisdiction of the subject matter, or of the parties, or acted in excess of its statutory authority.”

B. The Fraud Standard (Lewis v. Uselton)

Under Lewis v. Uselton, 202 Ga. App. 875 (1992), reliance on a signature known to be unauthorized constitutes extrinsic fraud sufficient to void a judgment.

Analysis: - McGhan had actual knowledge of attorney termination - McGhan knowingly used revoked credentials - The “Consent” signature was a spoofed authentication - The Order is a corrupted file with no legal validity

III. LEGAL ARGUMENT

A. Void Judgment Standard

A judgment is void when procured by fraud that prevents a party from fully presenting their case. O.C.G.A. § 9-11-60(d)(2). The fraud here is extrinsic - it occurred outside the adversarial process and prevented Defendant from having any representation at all.

B. “Zombie Authority” Doctrine

The continued use of terminated attorney credentials constitutes a “Man-in-the-Middle” attack on due process. Once authority is revoked, any subsequent actions using those credentials are legally null.

C. Jurisdictional Defect

The October 23, 2025 Order suffers from fundamental jurisdictional defects: 1. No valid party representation (terminated attorney) 2. Fraudulent authentication of consent 3. Violation of due process rights
