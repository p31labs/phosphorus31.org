V. LEGAL BASIS

A. ADA Title II Requirements Title II of the ADA requires state courts to: - Make reasonable modifications to policies, practices, and procedures when necessary to avoid discrimination - Ensure effective communication with individuals with disabilities - Provide auxiliary aids and services when necessary

B. Case Law Support Courts have recognized that reasonable accommodations may include: - Extended time for responses and decision-making - Written communication preferences - Modifications to standard procedures - Continuances to accommodate disability-related needs

See Tennessee v. Lane, 541 U.S. 509 (2004) (affirming ADA applies to courts)

VI. THESE ACCOMMODATIONS ARE REASONABLE

The requested accommodations: - Do not fundamentally alter the nature of court proceedings - Do not create undue burden on the court - Are standard accommodations provided to individuals with autism and ADHD - Will enable me to meaningfully participate in proceedings affecting my rights - Will reduce likelihood of misunderstandings and need for repeated proceedings
