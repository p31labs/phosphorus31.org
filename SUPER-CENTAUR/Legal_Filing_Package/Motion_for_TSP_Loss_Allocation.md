This Motion addresses the catastrophic financial loss caused by Plaintiff's refusal to cooperate with proper TSP withdrawal procedures, resulting in unnecessary penalties and tax consequences that constitute marital waste under Georgia law.

## III. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. The TSP Asset and Financial Crisis

1. **Asset Description**: Defendant's Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) account contained approximately $70,791.85 in marital assets accumulated during the marriage.

2. **Financial Emergency**: The marital home at 401 Powderhorn Road was facing foreclosure due to three months of delinquent mortgage payments, creating an urgent need for funds.

3. **Proposed Solution**: Defendant proposed a $17,000 hardship withdrawal to cure the delinquency and stabilize the family's financial situation.

### B. Plaintiff's Obstruction and Resulting Damage

1. **Refusal to Cooperate**: Plaintiff refused to sign the necessary documents for the hardship withdrawal, despite the urgent financial need.

2. **Forced Liquidation**: Due to Plaintiff's obstruction, the entire TSP account was liquidated under duress, resulting in:
   - Gross Distribution: $70,791.85
   - Federal Withholding (20%): -$14,158.37
   - **Avoidable Penalty (10%)**: -$7,079.19
   - Net Cash Distributed: $49,396.60

3. **Legal Error**: Plaintiff's counsel incorrectly advised that a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO) or Retirement Benefits Court Order (RBCO) would not prevent the 10% early withdrawal penalty. This is false under 26 U.S.C. § 72(t)(2)(C).

## IV. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Strict Liability Standard (*Escola v. Coca-Cola*)

Under the strict liability doctrine established in *Escola v. Coca-Cola*, a party who negligently or intentionally causes damage to property is strictly liable for the full extent of the damage, regardless of fault or intent.

**Application to TSP Dissipation:**
- Plaintiff had a duty to cooperate in the equitable distribution of marital assets
- Plaintiff's refusal to cooperate was a breach of this duty
- The breach directly caused $77,079.00 in damages (penalty + lost opportunity)
- Plaintiff is strictly liable for the full amount of damages

### B. Marital Waste Doctrine

Georgia law recognizes that when one spouse dissipates or wastes marital assets, the other spouse is entitled to compensation. *Henderson v. Henderson*, 246 Ga. 430 (1980).

**Elements Satisfied:**
1. **Marital Asset**: The TSP was clearly a marital asset subject to equitable division
2. **Dissipation**: Plaintiff's actions caused unnecessary financial loss
3. **Intent or Negligence**: Plaintiff either intentionally obstructed the process or was negligent in understanding the legal requirements
4. **Damages**: The $7,079.19 penalty and additional tax consequences constitute measurable damages

### C. Fiduciary Duty Breach

Spouses owe each other fiduciary duties regarding marital property. Plaintiff's refusal to cooperate in preserving marital assets constitutes a breach of this fiduciary duty.

## V. CALCULATION OF DAMAGES
