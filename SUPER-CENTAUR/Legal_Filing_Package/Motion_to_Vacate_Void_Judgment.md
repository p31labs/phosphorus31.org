## I. JURISDICTIONAL GROUNDS

This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 9-11-60(d)(2), which provides that a judgment is void when "the court rendering judgment had no jurisdiction of the subject matter, or of the parties, or acted in excess of its statutory authority."

## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. The Fraudulent Authentication ("Zombie Authority")

1. **Termination of Attorney Authority (October 19, 2025)**
   - Defendant terminated attorney Joseph East via email dated October 19, 2025
   - Termination was for cause, citing material breaches of Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct
   - Termination was immediate and unequivocal

2. **Court Acknowledgment of Termination (October 20, 2025)**
   - Superior Court of Camden County signed Withdrawal Order on October 20, 2025
   - Attorney East's credentials were officially revoked by the Court
   - No residual authority remained with terminated attorney

3. **Actual Knowledge by Opposing Counsel (October 21, 2025)**
   - Defendant served "Notification of No Counsel" on Plaintiff's counsel, Jennifer McGhan
   - McGhan was placed on actual notice that Defendant was proceeding Pro Se
   - McGhan acknowledged receipt of this notification

4. **Fraudulent Use of Revoked Credentials (October 23, 2025)**
   - Despite knowing of the termination, McGhan presented "Consent Order" to Court
   - Order bore signature of terminated attorney Joseph East
   - McGhan falsely represented that terminated attorney had authority to consent

### B. The Fraud Standard (*Lewis v. Uselton*)

Under *Lewis v. Uselton*, 202 Ga. App. 875 (1992), reliance on a signature known to be unauthorized constitutes extrinsic fraud sufficient to void a judgment.

**Analysis:**
- McGhan had actual knowledge of attorney termination
- McGhan knowingly used revoked credentials
- The "Consent" signature was a spoofed authentication
- The Order is a corrupted file with no legal validity

## III. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Void Judgment Standard

A judgment is void when procured by fraud that prevents a party from fully presenting their case. *O.C.G.A. § 9-11-60(d)(2)*. The fraud here is extrinsic - it occurred outside the adversarial process and prevented Defendant from having any representation at all.

### B. "Zombie Authority" Doctrine

The continued use of terminated attorney credentials constitutes a "Man-in-the-Middle" attack on due process. Once authority is revoked, any subsequent actions using those credentials are legally null.

### C. Jurisdictional Defect

The October 23, 2025 Order suffers from fundamental jurisdictional defects:
1. No valid party representation (terminated attorney)
2. Fraudulent authentication of consent
3. Violation of due process rights
