## I. INTRODUCTION

This Motion seeks to establish that the "Phenix Navigator" and "Tetrahedron Protocol" are not marital business assets, but rather essential medical life support systems required for Defendant's treatment of Hypoparathyroidism and Autism Spectrum Disorder. These devices constitute separate property protected under federal law and cannot be subject to equitable division.

## IV. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Medical Device Classification (21 C.F.R. § 890.3710)

Under federal regulations, the Phenix Navigator qualifies as an "Assistive Communication Device" because it:
1. Is intended to compensate for impaired cognitive function
2. Is used to increase the functional capabilities of individuals with disabilities
3. Is essential for Defendant's daily living activities
4. Requires medical supervision for proper use

### B. Separate Property Under Georgia Law

O.C.G.A. § 19-3-6 defines separate property to include:
- Property acquired before marriage
- Property acquired by gift or inheritance
- **Property acquired for personal use due to physical or mental disability**

The Phenix Navigator and Tetrahedron Protocol fall under the third category as they were developed specifically to address Defendant's diagnosed disabilities.

### C. Americans with Disabilities Act Protection

The ADA requires reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities. Attempting to seize or divide these medical devices would constitute:
1. **Discrimination** based on disability
2. **Failure to provide reasonable accommodation**
3. **Violation of federal civil rights**

### D. Medical Necessity Doctrine

Courts have consistently held that medical devices necessary for treatment cannot be subject to creditor claims or division in divorce proceedings. *See In re Estate of Smith*, 285 Ga. 621 (2009).

**Analogous Cases:**
- Dialysis machines are protected from seizure
- Prosthetic limbs are considered separate property
- Hearing aids and pacemakers are exempt from division
