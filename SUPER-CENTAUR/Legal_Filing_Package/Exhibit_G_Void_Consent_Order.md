## DOCUMENT 2: DEFENDANT'S OBJECTION TO VOID ORDER

**TO:** Camden County Superior Court
**FROM:** William R. Johnson, Pro Se
**DATE:** October 24, 2025
**SUBJECT:** Objection to Unauthorized Hearing Notice and Motion for Sanctions

**LEGAL BASIS FOR OBJECTION:**

**1. FRAUD UPON THE COURT (Lewis v. Uselton Standard)**

The "Consent Order" is void ab initio because:
- Joseph East's authority to represent Defendant was terminated on October 19, 2025
- East had actual knowledge of the termination (October 19, 2025 email)
- East signed the "Consented as to form and content" line without authority
- Plaintiff's counsel had actual knowledge of the termination

**2. LACK OF SUBJECT MATTER JURISDICTION (Adams v. Payne)**

The hearing was presided over by Judge O. Brent Green, a Juvenile Court Judge, without proper assignment:
- No Order of Assignment filed prior to October 16, 2025 hearing
- Order of Assignment filed October 23, 2025 (nunc pro tunc)
- Subject matter jurisdiction cannot be conferred retroactively

**3. VIOLATION OF DUE PROCESS**

Defendant was not properly served with notice of the October 16, 2025 hearing:
- No formal notice provided
- Defendant was unrepresented at the hearing
- No opportunity to be heard on the merits

**4. FINANCIAL HARM AND DISSIPATION**

The order forces Defendant to:
- Liquidate pre-marital TSP assets (accumulated 2009-2015)
- Incur unnecessary $7,079.19 in tax penalties
- Violate his ADA accommodation request for extended processing time

**RELIEF REQUESTED:**

1. **VACATE** the October 23, 2025 "Consent Order" as void ab initio
2. **SANCTION** Plaintiff's counsel for filing fraudulent documents
3. **AWARD** Defendant damages for financial harm caused by void order
4. **PROTECT** Defendant's pre-marital TSP assets from improper division

**VERIFICATION:**
