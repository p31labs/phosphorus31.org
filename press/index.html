One-Pager

P31 Labs

P31 Labs (Phosphorus-31) is a Georgia 501(c)(3) nonprofit in formation building open-source assistive technology for neurodivergent individuals. We create communication processing tools, sensory regulation devices, and executive function support systems that work offline, respect privacy, and form resilient mesh networks.

The Problem

15–20% of the global population is neurodivergent, yet there are zero open-source cognitive prosthetics. Commercial solutions are wellness apps with undefined FDA status, subscription fees, and cloud dependencies. They require smartphones, internet connections, and institutional permission — exactly what vulnerable populations often lack.

Our Solution

Our flagship product is Node One — an ESP32-S3-based handheld device for sensory regulation and off-grid communication. No internet required. No phone required. No permission required.

The Buffer — Communication processing layer that detects coercive patterns (urgency, coercion, shame, threats) before messages reach vulnerable recipients. Gives time to think. Time that was never given before.

The Scope — Unified executive function dashboard. Medication tracking, cognitive metrics, environmental sensing, mesh network status. Not a wellness app — a cognitive prosthetic.

Open Source by Default

All software is Apache 2.0 licensed. All hardware designs are CERN-OHL-S. All research is published to Zenodo with timestamped DOIs. We believe in defensive publication, not patents. Fork freely. Build on it. The mesh holds.

Built By Need

The founder is AuDHD, diagnosed at 39 after 16 years building workarounds that turned out to be assistive technology prototypes. Lived experience drives every design decision. Nothing about us without us.
