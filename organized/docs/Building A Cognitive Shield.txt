* Isotropy: The term "isotropic" means "everywhere the same." In the IVM, every vector (edge) is identical in length, and every vertex has the same relationship to its neighbors. Energy conditions are uniform in all directions.
* 60-Degree Logic: Stability in the physical universe is triangular. The IVM relies on 60-degree triangulation, which is inherently self-stabilizing. Forces are distributed axially rather than violently shearing as they do in 90-degree frames.3
