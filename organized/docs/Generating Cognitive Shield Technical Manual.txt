1. Introduction: The Geodesic Imperative
1.1 Overview: The Universal Translation Layer
Cognitive Shield represents a fundamental paradigm shift in human-computer interaction (HCI) and interpersonal communication, specifically engineered for neurodivergent cognition. It is not merely a messaging application or a filter; it is a Universal Translation Layer designed to decouple the emotional velocity of incoming signals from the cognitive load required to process them. Built upon the G.O.D. Protocol (Geodesic Operations/Governance), the system operationalizes R. Buckminster Fuller’s principles of synergetics and minimum structural systems to create a digital environment of "Harmonic Resonance."
In an era defined by the weaponization of information abundance and the extraction of attention capital, the neurodivergent mind often functions as a "canary in the coal mine," detecting signal volatility that neurotypical filters might ignore.1 The primary function of the Cognitive Shield is to act as an impedance matching transformer between the external world—characterized by chaotic, high-voltage emotional signals—and the internal state of the user. By interposing a processing layer governed by rigorous geometric and mathematical principles, the system allows neurodivergent individuals to navigate high-stakes communication without succumbing to "social proteopathy" (the toxic accumulation of misaligned social information) or "phase shift" collapse.3
The application acts as a sovereign digital territory. It is strictly local-first and privacy-focused, utilizing on-device AI (Ollama) to ensure that the user’s psychological data, emotional states, and private communications never leave their physical control. This "air-gapped" philosophy is not just a security feature; it is a psychological necessity, providing the "Topological Protection" required for the brain to maintain a "Mind Zone" free from external surveillance capitalism.3
1.2 The Target User: Who is the Shield For?
While the architectural principles of the Geodesic Engine are universal, the specific tuning of the Cognitive Shield is optimized for profiles that experience standard communication protocols as hostile or draining.
* The Neurodivergent Operator: Specifically those with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), ADHD, or sensory processing sensitivities. These individuals often face significant "cognitive overload" in standard interfaces due to a lack of customizable sensory gating.5 The Shield provides the "prosthetic filtering" that their biological systems may not automatically provide.
* High-Sensitivity Processors (HSP): Individuals whose "Cognitive Behavioral Barrier" (CBB) requires reinforcement against rapid emotional decoherence.3 For these users, a "medium voltage" email can trigger a "fight, flight, or freeze" response (amygdala hijack) that derails productivity for hours. The Shield intervenes before this physiological cascade begins.
* System Thinkers & Pattern Matchers: Users who require structural predictability and explicit rule sets to navigate social nuance. The Shield converts implied social contracts into explicit "HumanOS" parameters, turning baffling emotional subtext into parseable data.
* The Burned-Out Executive: Professionals suffering from decision fatigue who need an "Augmented Executive" layer to triage information flows and preserve "strategic judgment".1
1.3 Core Philosophy: Wye (Y) to Delta ($\Delta$) Topology
The foundational philosophy of the Cognitive Shield is derived from electrical engineering transformations, specifically the Wye-Delta (Y-$\Delta$) transform, applied metaphorically to social graph topology.6
The Wye (Star) Topology: The Pathology of Centralization
Traditional communication often resembles a Wye connection (or Star topology). In this configuration, all stressors, signals, and demands converge on a single central point—the neutral node.
* Electrical Analogy: In a balanced three-phase system, the neutral point is stable. However, if the loads are unbalanced (as human emotions invariably are), the neutral point carries significant current.
* Social Analogy: The neurodivergent individual often acts as this "neutral point" in a family or work dynamic. They absorb the full volatility of the network. When the "social voltage" spikes (conflict, urgency, ambiguity), the central node experiences thermal overload, leading to burnout, shutdown, or what quantum consciousness theorists might call a "Massive Synchronous Global Phase Shift"—a total system collapse.3
The Delta Topology: The Architecture of Stability
The Cognitive Shield transforms this topology into a Delta ($\Delta$) configuration.
* Electrical Analogy: In a Delta system, loads are distributed across the phases (the edges of the triangle) rather than converging on a central point. There is no neutral line to overload. The "line voltage" is equal to the "phase voltage," but the "line current" is split, reducing the stress on any single component to $1/\sqrt{3}$ (approximately 58%) of the original load.7
* The Solution: The Shield distributes the cognitive load across a 4-node geometric structure (The Tetrahedron). No single node bears the full weight of the interaction. The user (Node A) is never directly connected to the stressor (Node B). The signal must pass through Context (Node C) and the AI Engine (Node D). This geometric buffering ensures that the emotional "current" is stepped down before it touches the user's perception.

2. Quick Start Guide
This section outlines the initialization sequence for the Cognitive Shield. As a local-first application, setup requires ensuring your "node" (computer) is prepared to host the Geodesic Engine.
2.1 Prerequisites & Installation
The Shield runs on a standard React/TypeScript stack but relies on a local LLM for its intelligence.
System Requirements:
* OS: macOS (Apple Silicon recommended for AI speed), Linux, or Windows (WSL2).
* RAM: Minimum 8GB, 16GB+ recommended for smooth 8-bit quantization of Llama-3.
* Storage: ~10GB free space for model weights and vector logs.
Step 1: Clone and Install
Access the repository (simulated as trimtab-signal/cognitive-shield) and hydrate the dependencies.


Bash




# Clone the repository
git clone https://github.com/trimtab-signal/cognitive-shield.git

# Navigate to the directory
cd cognitive-shield

# Install Node dependencies (using npm or bun)
npm install

Step 2: Initialize Environment
The system requires a .env.local file to define security boundaries.


Bash




# Create environment file
touch.env.local

Add the following configuration:


Code snippet




# Allow local browser access to the AI API
OLLAMA_ORIGINS="http://localhost:3000"

# Enable Vibe Coding module (Creator Mode)
VIBE_CODER_ENABLED=true

# Set default resonance target (Mark 1 Attractor)
HARMONIC_TARGET=0.35

2.2 Setting Up Ollama (The Geodesic Engine)
The "brain" of Node D is Ollama, a tool for running LLMs locally. This ensures no data leaves your machine—a critical requirement for the "Cognitive Shield" privacy model.
1. Download Ollama: Visit ollama.com or run:
Bash
curl -fsSL https://ollama.com/install.sh | sh

2. Pull the Core Model: The Shield is optimized for llama3 due to its balance of reasoning and speed.
Bash
ollama pull llama3

3. Start the Engine:
Bash
ollama serve

Troubleshooting: If you see "address already in use," ensure no other docker containers are binding port 11434.
2.3 First Run: The Pre-Launch Sequence
Once the dependencies are installed and the AI is listening:
   1. Launch the Interface:
Bash
npm run dev

   2. Navigate: Open your browser to http://localhost:3000.
   3. Calibration: You will be greeted by the PreLaunchSequence.tsx.
      * Step A: "Check Geometry." The app verifies it can render the 3D Tetrahedron.
      * Step B: "Ping Geodesic Engine." The app sends a test prompt ("Define Stability") to Ollama to verify the local link.
      * Step C: "Baseline Somatics." You will be asked to breathe in sync with a visual guide to establish a baseline Heartbeat.
Once all checks pass, the "Hub is offline. The Mesh is operational" message appears, and you enter the main dashboard.

3. User Guide: Operations
The Cognitive Shield is not a passive inbox; it is an active console for managing information flow.
3.1 The Daily Heartbeat Check-in
Before processing any external signals, the user must calibrate their internal node (Node A). This is the Heartbeat Protocol.
      1. The Interface: Upon login, the HeartbeatPanel.tsx overlays the screen. It presents a simple, non-judgmental slider (0-100%) and a query: "What is your current capacity?"
      2. The Assessment: The user reflects on their "Spoons" (metabolic energy).8
      * Are you in pain?
      * Did you sleep?
      * Is sensory overload present?
      3. System Adaptation: The Shield adjusts its "Gating Logic" based on this input:
      * > 80% (Green/Resonant): "Systems Nominal." No filtering. Complex replies enabled. Module Maker unlocked.
      * 50-80% (Yellow/Conservation): "Conservation Mode." Background animations paused to reduce visual load. "Reply Complex" creates a draft but warns about cost.
      * 25-50% (Orange/Defensive): "Defensive Mode." The "Catchers Mitt" buffer expands. High-voltage messages (>6) are automatically queued.
      * < 25% (Red/Lockdown): "Deep Processing Lock." The message input is disabled. The "Inbox" is hidden. Only Somatic Regulation tools are accessible. The system effectively says, "You are not safe to process right now. Regulate first."
3.2 Processing Messages: The Shield Workflow
When a message arrives (or is pasted in), it undergoes the Shield Processing Pipeline.
      1. Ingestion: The raw text enters via MessageInput.tsx.
      2. Interception (The Catcher's Mitt): The text does not appear on screen immediately. It is caught by the CatchersMitt.tsx component. A loading animation (a spinning tetrahedron) indicates Node D is analyzing.
      3. Analysis:
      * Voltage: The AI scores emotional intensity (0-10).
      * Spoon Cost: The AI estimates the metabolic cost to read and reply (e.g., "Cost: 3 Spoons").
      * HumanOS: The AI profiles the sender (e.g., "Sender appears to be: The Guardian").
      4. Presentation (The Payload Card):
      * If Safe (Voltage < 4): The message is displayed normally.
      * If High Voltage (> 7): The user sees a warning card: "High Energy Signal Detected. Voltage: 8/10. Spoon Cost: High. Do you want to process this now?"
      5. Translation: If the user proceeds, they see the Translated Payload first.
      * Raw: "Why haven't you finished this? It's urgent and you're delaying the whole team!"
      * Translated: "The sender is expressing anxiety about the project timeline (The Achiever profile). They are requesting a status update to alleviate their fear of blocking the team."
      * Purpose: This strips the "accusatory" tone, allowing the neurodivergent user to process the information without triggering Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD).
3.3 Somatic Regulation Tools
If the Heartbeat is low, or a message triggers a spike, the user navigates to the Somatic Regulation panel.
      * Lime Drag (Trigeminal Reset):
      * Theory: Strong sensory input (sourness, cold) stimulates the trigeminal nerve, disrupting the "fight or flight" loop.10
      * Action: The screen displays a high-contrast, slow-moving "lime" texture. The user is prompted to physically engage (e.g., "Chew something sour") and drag their mouse through the resistance of the virtual lime. The visual "lag" forces the visual cortex to slow down, entraining the heart rate.
      * Grounding Rose:
      * Theory: Based on the "5-4-3-2-1" technique and aromatherapy associations (Rose/Camphor) for protection.11
      * Action: A geometric rose blooms in sync with 4-7-8 breathing. The user clicks a petal for each sensory input they identify in their physical room ("I see a lamp," "I feel the chair"). The petals only unfold if the rhythm is respected.
      * Heavy Work Tracker:
      * Theory: Proprioceptive input (pushing, pulling) releases serotonin and dopamine, calming the nervous system.13
      * Action: A gamified log where users record "Wall Pushes" or "Weighted Blanket Time." Logging these activities "refills" the virtual Spoons in the heartbeat.store.
3.4 Creating Modules: The Vibe Coder
For advanced users, the Shield is extensible via Vibe Coding.15
      1. Open ModuleMaker.tsx.
      2. Prompt: "I want a widget that tracks the moon phase and blocks messages during the full moon."
      3. Generate: The local LLM writes the React component.
      4. Abdication: Once the tool works, the user clicks "Abdicate." The code is locked and becomes a permanent, read-only part of the system's "Nature," preventing the user from endlessly tweaking it (a common neurodivergent trap).

4.2 State Management (Zustand Stores)
We utilize Zustand for state management due to its minimal boilerplate and transient update capabilities, which are essential for handling high-frequency "voltage" fluctuations without causing unnecessary React re-renders.
shield.store.ts
The central nervous system of the app.
      * rawMessage: String. The unprocessed input.
      * voltage: Number (0-10). The emotional intensity score.
      * curvature: Number. The Ollivier-Ricci curvature metric of the conversation thread.
      * processedPayload: Object. Contains the translated text, summary, and HumanOS profile.
      * humanOS: Enum. The detected archetype of the sender.
heartbeat.store.ts
The biological resource monitor.
      * currentSpoons: Integer (0-12). Based on Miserandino’s Spoon Theory.
      * heartbeatStatus: Percentage (0-100%).
      * deepProcessingLock: Boolean. True if heartbeatStatus < 25.
      * lastReset: Timestamp. Used to calculate daily spoon replenishment.
module.store.ts
The registry of user-created extensions.
      * activeModules: Array of Module Objects.
      * abdicatedModules: Array of IDs. Modules that have been locked via the Abdication Principle.
4.3 Data Flow Diagrams
The Ingestion Path:
Raw Input -> shield.store (update rawMessage) -> GeodesicEngine (Trigger Analysis) -> Ollama API -> Analysis Result -> shield.store (update voltage, payload) -> CatchersMitt (Render Decision)
The Regulation Path:
Somatic Action (e.g., Wall Push) -> SomaticRegulation Component -> heartbeat.store (increment currentSpoons) -> HeartbeatPanel (Update Visuals) -> Gating Logic (Unlock Deep Queue?)

6. Mathematical Foundation
The Cognitive Shield is distinguished by its reliance on invariant mathematical properties to ensure stability. It moves beyond "subjective" therapy tools into "objective" geometric regulation.
6.1 The Tetrahedron Protocol
R. Buckminster Fuller identified the tetrahedron as the "minimum structural system" in the universe. A system requires four nodes to enclose volume and maintain structural integrity independent of external support.19
      * Application: In the Shield, no dyadic (2-node) communication is permitted. A dyad (You vs. Them) is unstable and collapses into a line of opposition. The Shield triangulates every interaction through Node C (Context) and Node D (AI), creating a stable volume. This "structural quantum" resists the entropy of misunderstanding.
6.2 SIC-POVM Symmetry
To ensure the AI (Node D) provides an unbiased translation, the system utilizes SIC-POVM (Symmetric Informationally Complete Positive Operator-Valued Measure).4
      * Concept: A set of quantum measurements that have equal pairwise overlap (symmetry) and allow for total state reconstruction.
      * Implementation: The Shield's parsing algorithm aims for an "informational overlap" of exactly 1/3 between the user's interpretation, the sender's intent, and the AI's analysis. This mathematical symmetry prevents any single perspective (especially the user's anxiety or the sender's aggression) from dominating the reality frame. It enforces a "fair" distribution of truth.
6.3 Harmonic Resonance (0.35 Attractor)
The system targets a specific stability coefficient known as the Harmonic Resonance Attractor, set at approximately 0.35 (or $\approx \pi/9$).17
      * The Nexus Framework: Research into recursive harmonic systems suggests that sustainable systems gravitate toward a potential-vs-actualization ratio of ~35%. This is the "Mark 1 Attractor"—the boundary between rigid order (crystallization) and chaotic turbulence.17
      * The Harmonic Linter: When drafting a response, the Shield analyzes the text.
      * Score < 0.2: Indicates passivity, depression, or "fawning" (over-smoothing).
      * Score > 0.5: Indicates aggression, mania, or high entropy.
      * Target 0.35: The system nudges the user to edit until the resonance score approaches 0.35, ensuring the response is firm yet regulated.
6.4 Ollivier-Ricci Curvature
The system calculates the Ollivier-Ricci Curvature of the conversation graph to detect structural pathologies in the information flow.24
      * Positive Curvature (+): Indicates "Over-Smoothing." The conversation is stuck in a cluster of redundant, self-reinforcing messages (echo chamber). The Shield suggests: "Break the loop. Introduce new context."
      * Negative Curvature (-): Indicates "Over-Squashing." The conversation is trying to force too much information through a bottleneck (a bridge edge), leading to congestion and misunderstanding. The Shield suggests: "Decompress. Split into smaller topics."

8. Troubleshooting
8.1 Ollama Connection Issues
      * Symptom: "Geodesic Engine Offline" or infinite loading.
      * Fix:
      1. Verify Ollama is running: curl http://localhost:11434.
      2. CORS Error: Inspect browser console. If Cross-Origin Request Blocked, stop Ollama and restart with: OLLAMA_ORIGINS="*" ollama serve.
      3. Model Mismatch: Ensure god.config.ts calls llama3 and that ollama list shows llama3.
8.2 Build Errors
      * Heap Out of Memory: Vibe coding creates large ASTs. Increase Node memory:
export NODE_OPTIONS="--max-old-space-size=4096"
      * Tailscale Funnel 403: Ensure the Shield is listening on localhost (not 127.0.0.1) if specific binding is required by the funnel.
8.3 "Brain Rot" Loop
         * Symptom: User is stuck checking the Heartbeat or tweaking modules without processing messages.
         * Fix: The heartbeat.store has an "Inertia Breaker." If interaction > 15 mins without message processing, the Shield triggers a "Pattern Interrupt" (usually a sudden Lime Drag prompt).
