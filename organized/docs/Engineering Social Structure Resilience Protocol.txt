Part II: The Geometry of Resilience
2.1 The Case for the Tetrahedron (K4)
To build a resilient distributed system, one must define the "Minimum Structural Unit." The research validates the Tetrahedron (K4 Complete Graph) as this unit.2
Why not a triangle (3)? In 2D space, a triangle is stable. However, humans exist in multidimensional social space. A group of three often devolves into "two against one" dynamics (the triad instability). Furthermore, a triangle has no volume—it cannot "enclose" space or protect what is inside.2
Why not a square (4)? A square frame is structurally unstable; it collapses into a rhombus under stress unless verified by a diagonal. In social groups, a "four" without diagonal connections creates distance between opposite pairs.2
The Tetrahedron connects 4 points with 6 edges. Every point is directly connected to every other point. It is the simplest possible structure that creates a strictly rigid system in 3D space. As Fuller noted in Synergetics, it is the "minimum structural system of the Universe".2
Properties of the K4 Social Unit:
* Redundancy: If one vertex (person) is lost to death, divorce, or departure, the remaining three still form a triangle (a stable 2D shape). The structure does not collapse; it merely loses volume, allowing it to survive the transition while recruiting a replacement.3
* Maximum Intimacy / Minimum Complexity: A group of 4 creates 6 relationships. A group of 5 creates 10 relationships. As the group size grows linearly, the complexity of relationships grows geometrically (Combinatorial Explosion). The Tetrahedron represents the upper limit of a group that can maintain "high-trust" peer-to-peer connection without needing a leader to manage the communication overhead.1

* Mitigation: Multi-Sig Attestation. The Constitution requires a cryptographic handshake via short-range transport (Bluetooth LE / NFC). This proves proximity, not just location. Three devices must cryptographically sign that they "saw" each other. This is much harder to spoof than GPS.3

* Risk: Apple or Google bans the app because it refuses to include backdoors for law enforcement (violating the Constitution).
* Mitigation: Client Agnosticism. The Protocol Specification (Layer 4) must be public. If the "Official App" is banned, the community can build "G.O.D. Libre" or run terminal clients that interact with the same smart contracts. The network survives the death of the client.3
5.3 The Deadlock (Governance Stagnation)
* Risk: A requirement for 66% supermajority in the DAO can lead to stagnation if voter apathy is high.
* Mitigation: Quadratic Voting & The Council. Implementation of Quadratic Voting to prevent "whale" dominance, and the use of randomly selected "Guardian Nodes" (sortition) to break ties and vet proposals, ensuring the system can evolve without being captured.3

* Mitigation: The "Canary" Check. The audit-constitutional-compliance.sh script must scan the build for opaque binaries (.a, .so, .dll). The build fails if non-open-source code is detected. "Transparency is not a policy; it is a compile-time constraint".3
