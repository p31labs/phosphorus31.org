3.1 The Buckminster Fuller Lineage
The concept is derived directly from R. Buckminster Fuller, a primary intellectual influence on Johnson. In naval architecture, the rudder of a massive ship is subjected to immense hydrodynamic pressure. Turning the rudder directly requires enormous energy, often more than the engine can provide during a turn. To solve this, engineers attach a tiny miniature rudder—the trim tab—to the trailing edge of the main rudder.
By applying a small amount of force to the trim tab, a low-pressure zone is created. This pressure differential "sucks" the massive main rudder into the desired position, which in turn steers the colossal ship. Fuller famously said, "Call me Trimtab," signifying that a single individual, by applying precise pressure at the critical point of leverage, can change the course of civilization.
