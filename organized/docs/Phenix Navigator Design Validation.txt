2. Quantum Information Theory and Measurement Protocols
The foundational layer of the Sorcery architecture is rooted in the extraction of information from quantum systems. Unlike classical bits, quantum states are fragile and continuous. Their characterization requires measurement protocols that balance information gain with state disturbance. The theoretical gold standard for such measurement is the Symmetric Informationally Complete Positive Operator-Valued Measure (SIC-POVM).
2.1 The Geometry of Quantum Measurement: SIC-POVMs
To understand the operational necessity of SIC-POVMs in the Sorcery architecture, one must first appreciate the limitations of standard von Neumann measurements. A standard projective measurement (PVM) on a $d$-dimensional Hilbert space has $d$ outcomes corresponding to an orthogonal basis. For a qubit ($d=2$), this yields two outcomes (e.g., $|0\rangle$ and $|1\rangle$). However, a general quantum state is described by a density matrix $\rho$, which has $d^2 - 1$ independent real parameters (plus a normalization constraint). For a qubit, $\rho$ requires 3 parameters to be fully specified. A single PVM, providing only $d-1$ independent probabilities, is therefore "informationally incomplete." It cannot uniquely determine the state.1
2.1.1 The Tetrahedral Geometry in the Bloch Sphere
A SIC-POVM solves this deficit by introducing a generalized measurement with exactly $d^2$ outcomes. For a qubit, this means $2^2 = 4$ outcomes. These outcomes are defined by rank-1 projectors $\Pi_i = |\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i|$ that possess a high degree of symmetry. In the geometric representation of the qubit state space—the Bloch Sphere—the vectors corresponding to these four outcomes form the vertices of a Regular Tetrahedron.1
This geometric configuration is not arbitrary; it represents the maximal separation of measurement vectors in the state space. The defining characteristic of a SIC-POVM is that the overlap (Hilbert-Schmidt inner product) between any two distinct elements is constant. Mathematically, the condition is:


$$\text{Tr}(\Pi_i \Pi_j) = \frac{d\delta_{ij} + 1}{d+1}$$
For the qubit case ($d=2$), this simplifies to a constant overlap of $\text{Tr}(\Pi_i \Pi_j) = \frac{1}{3}$ for all $i \neq j$.1 This symmetry ensures that the measurement extracts information "democratically" from all sectors of the Hilbert space, minimizing the statistical bias inherent in selecting a specific measurement axis.
2.1.2 Informational Completeness and State Reconstruction
The property of Informational Completeness implies that the statistics collected from a SIC-POVM are sufficient to reconstruct the density matrix $\rho$ entirely. If $p_i = \text{Tr}(\rho \Pi_i)$ is the probability of obtaining outcome $i$, the state can be reconstructed via a linear inversion:


$$\rho = \sum_{i=1}^{d^2} \left[ (d+1)p_i - 1 \right] \Pi_i$$
This elegant inversion formula is a direct consequence of the symmetric properties of the measurement vectors. In the context of the Sorcery system, this allows the quantum sensor node to maintain a real-time estimate of the quantum channel's fidelity without needing to switch between mutually unbiased bases (MUBs) mechanically or optically, significantly reducing the complexity of the "Tetrahedron Protocol" hardware.4
2.2 The "Tetrahedron Protocol" and Self-Calibrating Tomography (SCT)
While the theory of SIC-POVMs is robust, its practical implementation is challenging due to the precision required in alignment. The "Tetrahedron Protocol" leverages the geometry of SIC-POVMs to perform Self-Calibrating Quantum State Tomography (SCT), a technique that simultaneously recovers the quantum state and characterizes the errors in the measurement apparatus itself.
2.2.1 Experimental Realization via Birefringent Optics
Experimental demonstrations of this protocol utilize polarization-encoded qubits. The setup typically involves the generation of photon pairs via Spontaneous Parametric Down-Conversion (SPDC). A high-energy pump photon (e.g., 405nm) incident on a non-linear crystal (like Beta-Barium Borate, BBO) splits into two entangled photons.2
In the "black box" scenario addressed by SCT, the unitary operations used to rotate the measurement basis are imperfectly known. For instance, a wave plate intended to rotate polarization by $45^\circ$ might actually rotate it by $43^\circ$ due to manufacturing tolerances or thermal drift. The SCT protocol mitigates this by applying a sequence of rotations that trace out the tetrahedral geometry.
* The Operator Set: The protocol applies rotations $R(\alpha)$, $R(-\alpha)$, $R(2\alpha)$, and $R(-2\alpha)$, where $\alpha$ is the unknown retardance of a Birefringent Polymer Sheet (BPS) or Liquid Crystal Wave Plate (LCWP).2
* Redundancy and Estimation: By measuring the photon counts for this specific set of rotations, the system generates an over-complete dataset. The symmetry of the rotations allows the analysis algorithm to solve for the unknown parameter $\alpha$ while simultaneously estimating the density matrix elements.
* Fidelity: Experimental results have shown that this method yields an average fidelity of $\bar{F} = 0.99 \pm 0.01$ when compared to standard tomography (which assumes perfect knowledge of the apparatus).2 This proves that the "Sorcery" node can operate reliably in harsh environments where optical calibration might drift.
2.2.2 Relation to Spherical t-Designs and QKD
The vectors forming a SIC-POVM also constitute a complex projective 2-design. This means that averaging a polynomial function of degree 2 over the SIC-POVM elements is equivalent to averaging over the entire unitary group measure (Haar measure).1
This property is exploited in the Singapore Protocol for Quantum Key Distribution (QKD). In this protocol, the minimal information leakage implied by the SIC-POVM structure allows Alice and Bob to detect eavesdropping (Eve) with maximal sensitivity. Since the SIC-POVM touches the "extremal" points of the state space symmetrically, any disturbance introduced by Eve distorts the correlations in a specific, detectable pattern.5 For the Sorcery system, implementing the Singapore Protocol ensures that the communication channel between the quantum sensor and the central processor is information-theoretically secure.

3. Advanced Power Network Topologies: Wye-Delta Architectures
While the quantum layer manages information at the photon level, the Sorcery system must also interface with and survive within the high-energy environment of electrical power distribution. The topology of choice for robust, versatile power networks is the interaction between Wye (Star, Y) and Delta ($\Delta$, Mesh) configurations.
3.1 Mathematical Formalism of Impedance Transformations
The analysis of complex power grids is often simplified by converting between Wye and Delta topologies. This equivalence allows a network designer to replace a "Pi" ($\Pi$) loop of impedances with a "Tee" ($T$) node without altering the terminal behavior of the circuit.6
3.1.1 The Delta-to-Wye Transformation ($\Delta \rightarrow Y$)
Consider a Delta configuration with three impedances $R_a, R_b, R_c$ connecting terminals A-B, B-C, and C-A respectively. We wish to replace this with a Wye configuration with impedances $R_1, R_2, R_3$ connecting terminals A, B, C to a common neutral point $N$. The transformation is derived by equating the impedance between any two terminals (e.g., A and B) in both networks while the third terminal (C) is open-circuited.
The impedance $R_1$ connected to terminal A is given by the product of the two adjacent Delta impedances divided by the sum of all three:




$$R_1 = \frac{R_b R_c}{R_a + R_b + R_c}$$


Similarly,




$$R_2 = \frac{R_a R_c}{R_a + R_b + R_c}, \quad R_3 = \frac{R_a R_b}{R_a + R_b + R_c}$$
Insight: This transformation reveals that the equivalent Wye impedances are generally smaller than their Delta counterparts. In a perfectly balanced system where $R_a = R_b = R_c = R_\Delta$, the Wye impedance becomes exactly one-third of the Delta impedance: $R_Y = R_\Delta / 3$. This scaling factor is crucial for the Sorcery system's "Phantom Mechanism" simulation, where virtual impedances are dynamically switched to model load variations.6
3.1.2 The Wye-to-Delta Transformation ($Y \rightarrow \Delta$)
Conversely, transforming a Wye network into a Delta network involves summing the products of all impedance pairs and dividing by the opposite impedance. For the impedance $R_a$ (connecting B and C, opposite to A):




$$R_a = \frac{R_1 R_2 + R_2 R_3 + R_3 R_1}{R_1}$$


This formula is essential for fault modeling. If a neutral connection in a Wye network breaks (infinite impedance), the system effectively degrades into a simplified series topology. However, the $Y \rightarrow \Delta$ transform allows the simulation of a "virtual" Delta network that represents the inter-phase coupling remaining in the system.6
3.2 Vector Groups and Phase Displacement
In AC power systems, the connection of transformers in Wye or Delta introduces phase shifts between the primary and secondary voltages. This phenomenon is categorized by the transformer's Vector Group.7
3.2.1 The 30-Degree Shift Mechanism
A standard configuration is the Delta-Wye (Dy) transformer. Here, the primary windings are connected in Delta, and the secondary windings in Wye.
* Phase Voltage vs. Line Voltage: In the Wye secondary, the line-to-neutral voltage $V_{ln}$ is the phase voltage. The line-to-line voltage $V_{ll}$ is the vector difference of two phase voltages: $\vec{V}_{ab} = \vec{V}_{an} - \vec{V}_{bn}$.
* Vector Geometry: Because $\vec{V}_{bn}$ is shifted by $120^\circ$ relative to $\vec{V}_{an}$, the subtraction yields a resultant vector with magnitude $\sqrt{3}|\vec{V}_{an}|$ and a phase shift of $30^\circ$.
* Implication: Depending on the wiring polarity, the secondary line voltage will either lead or lag the primary line voltage by $30^\circ$. A Dy1 vector group implies a $30^\circ$ lag (1 o'clock position), while Dy11 implies a $30^\circ$ lead (11 o'clock position).7 The Sorcery monitoring node must account for this shift when comparing phasor measurements across a transformer; failure to compensate results in erroneous differential current readings and false grid trips.
3.3 The Phenomenon of Floating Neutrals
A critical failure mode in Wye-configured systems is the "Floating Neutral." This occurs when the neutral return path to the source is severed or omitted in an ungrounded system.7
3.3.1 Voltage Instability Mechanism
In a balanced Wye system, the vector sum of currents at the neutral point is zero ($I_a + I_b + I_c = 0$), and the neutral potential $V_n$ remains at zero (ground potential). However, if the loads are unbalanced and the neutral path is open:
* Neutral Shift: The neutral point potential $V_n$ is no longer fixed at ground. It "floats" to a position defined by the load admittances $Y_a, Y_b, Y_c$:

$$V_n = \frac{V_a Y_a + V_b Y_b + V_c Y_c}{Y_a + Y_b + Y_c}$$
* Consequence: The phase-to-neutral voltages become severely distorted. A lightly loaded phase (small $Y$, high $R$) will experience a massive voltage swell, potentially rising to the full line-to-line voltage ($\sqrt{3} V_{nom}$), destroying equipment. Conversely, a heavily loaded phase will see a voltage sag.8
* Virtual Neutrals: To mitigate this in Delta systems (which lack a physical neutral), a Zig-Zag Transformer or a grounding bank is used to create a low-impedance path for zero-sequence currents, effectively creating a "Virtual Neutral" to stabilize the system.8
3.4 Network Resilience Benchmarks: The 57.7% Threshold

4. Embedded Hardware Architecture: The ESP32-S3 Core
The physical embodiment of the Sorcery node is built upon the ESP32-S3 System-on-Chip (SoC). Specifically, the implementation utilizes the Waveshare ESP32-S3-Touch-LCD-3.5B development board. 4.1 Processor and Computational Pipeline
The ESP32-S3 is a dual-core Xtensa® 32-bit LX7 microprocessor, designed specifically for AIoT (Artificial Intelligence of Things) applications.
   * Processing Power: The cores are clocked at up to 240 MHz. The LX7 architecture includes specialized vector instructions (AI acceleration) that significantly speed up neural network inference and signal processing tasks (like FFTs for power harmonic analysis).12
   * Memory Architecture:
   * Internal SRAM: 512 KB, used for instruction cache and critical data stacks.
   * Internal ROM: 384 KB, housing the bootloader and Bluetooth stack.
   * External PSRAM (Pseudo-Static RAM): The Waveshare Type B board integrates 8 MB of Octal SPI PSRAM. This is critical for the Sorcery application. The 3.5-inch LCD (320x480 resolution) requires a large framebuffer (approx. 300KB for 16-bit color), and the AI models (WakeNet, MultiNet) require several megabytes of working memory. Without this 8MB expansion, the system could not support the "Sorcery" voice assistant.12
   * External Flash: 16 MB NOR Flash (W25Q128JVSIQ) provides ample storage for the firmware, the 123 haptic waveforms, graphical assets (fonts, icons), and the HTML/JS for the MCP server interface.12
4.2 Display and User Interface Subsystem
The Human-Machine Interface (HMI) is centered around a high-fidelity touch display.
   * Display Panel: A 3.5-inch IPS (In-Plane Switching) LCD.
   * Resolution: 320 $\times$ 480 pixels.
   * Color Depth: 262K colors (RGB666 format usually mapped to RGB565 for SPI efficiency).
   * Controller: The panel is driven by an AXS15231B or similar controller via a QSPI (Quad SPI) interface. QSPI allows 4 bits of data transfer per clock cycle, enabling high frame rates (>30 FPS) necessary for smooth animations of the quantum Bloch sphere.12
   * Touch Interface: A capacitive touch overlay is managed by an FT6336 (or similar) controller. This chip communicates via I2C and supports multi-touch gestures (pinch-to-zoom for analyzing detailed Wye-Delta phasor diagrams).14
4.3 Audio and Power Subsystems
To support the voice interaction features of the "Sorcery" persona, the board includes a sophisticated audio path.
   * Audio Codec: The ES8311 is a low-power, mono audio codec. It handles the Digital-to-Analog conversion for the speaker output and Analog-to-Digital conversion for the microphone input.12
   * Data Interface: Audio data is transferred via I2S (Inter-IC Sound).
   * Control Interface: Configuration (volume, gain) is handled via I2C.
   * Power Management: The system is powered by an AXP2101 Power Management IC (PMIC). This highly integrated chip manages the Li-Po battery charging (via MX1.25 connector), regulates the 3.3V system rail, and provides multiple LDO outputs for peripherals. It communicates battery status (voltage, charge level) to the ESP32 via I2C, allowing the Sorcery system to report its own "health".12

5. Sensory Augmentation: The Haptic Feedback Loop
To transcend visual data, the Sorcery system employs haptic feedback to confirm inputs and alert the user to invisible system states (e.g., quantum decoherence or voltage drift).
5.1 Actuator Physics: LRA vs. ERM
Haptic actuators generally fall into two categories: Eccentric Rotating Mass (ERM) and Linear Resonant Actuator (LRA).
   * ERM: A DC motor with an off-center mass. Simple to drive, but slow start/stop times resulting in "mushy" feedback.
   * LRA: A magnetic mass suspended on a spring, driven by an AC voice coil. LRAs are superior for the Sorcery application because they offer rapid start/stop times (crisp clicks) and high efficiency at resonance. However, they require a precise AC drive signal matched to their resonant frequency (typically 175-235 Hz).18
5.2 The DRV2605L Driver and Smart-Loop Architecture
The Texas Instruments DRV2605L is the selected driver, chosen for its ability to intelligently manage LRA characteristics via its Smart-Loop Architecture.18
   * Auto-Resonance Tracking: The resonant frequency of an LRA shifts with mounting position, temperature, and age. The DRV2605L continuously measures the Back-EMF of the actuator to detect the resonance zero-crossing. It dynamically adjusts the drive frequency to maintain resonance, ensuring maximum vibration strength (G-force) regardless of environmental conditions.
   * Overdrive and Braking: To create a "sharp" click sensation, the actuator must reach full amplitude instantly and stop instantly.
   * Overdrive: The driver applies a voltage higher than the rated voltage for a few milliseconds to accelerate the mass.
   * Braking: The driver applies a reverse-phase signal to actively cancel the mass's momentum. This reduces the "ringing" tail of the vibration, making a 50ms pulse feel like a distinct mechanical switch event.18
5.3 Waveform Sequencing and Psychophysics
The DRV2605L includes a licensed version of the Immersion TouchSense 2200 library, containing 123 pre-designed waveforms.
   * Psychophysics of Click: Human tactile perception is highly sensitive to the temporal envelope of vibration. A "Strong Click" (Effect #1) mimics the force profile of a mechanical dome switch. A "Double Click" (Effect #10) consists of two sharp pulses separated by a silence interval optimized for temporal discrimination.19
   * Information Encoding: The Sorcery system maps specific waveforms to system states:
   * Effect 1 (Strong Click): Confirmation of voice command receipt.
   * Effect 47 (Buzz): Continuous feedback during "Thinking" phase of the DeepSeek R1 model.
   * Effect 58 (Transition): Ramping vibration indicating a Wye-Delta topology switch.
   * Implementation: The ESP32 triggers these effects via I2C commands. The driver has an internal sequencer that can chain up to 8 waveforms, offloading the timing burden from the main processor.19

The cognitive layer transforms the Sorcery node from a passive tool into an active assistant. This is achieved through a hybrid AI architecture combining local edge processing (ESP-Skainet) and cloud-based reasoning (DeepSeek).
6.1 The DeepSeek API: V3 vs. R1
The DeepSeek Large Language Model (LLM) provides the reasoning engine. The API offers two distinct models, each serving a specific role in the architecture.21
   * DeepSeek-V3 (deepseek-chat): This is a general-purpose model optimized for speed and cost.
   * Role: Handling routine queries ("What is the current voltage?"), conversational chit-chat, and formatting JSON outputs for tool calls.
   * Economics: Extremely cost-effective at $0.14 per million tokens (cached). This allows the Sorcery system to maintain a continuous dialogue context without draining budget.
   * DeepSeek-R1 (deepseek-reasoner): This is a specialized model capable of Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning.
   * Role: Complex diagnostics. When the system detects a 57.7% anomaly in the network topology or a drop in SIC-POVM fidelity, R1 is engaged.
   * Thinking Process: R1 can generate up to 32,000 tokens of internal "thought" before producing an output. It can systematically rule out hypotheses (e.g., "Is it a sensor fault or a real power surge?") step-by-step.
   * Cost: significantly higher ($0.55/M input, $2.19/M output). Therefore, the system logic reserves R1 for high-priority alerts.21
6.2 Context Caching and Prompt Engineering
To run an LLM effectively on an embedded device with limited bandwidth, Context Caching is essential.
   * Mechanism: The DeepSeek API caches the prefix of a prompt. If multiple requests share the same initial text (e.g., the system prompt defining the "Sorcery" persona and the hardware definition), the API does not re-process those tokens.
   * Strategy: The Sorcery firmware constructs the prompt structure:
+ + [User Query].
Since the System Prompt and Hardware State structure remains largely static, the system achieves a high cache hit rate, reducing latency (Time-To-First-Token) and cost.21
6.3 The Model Context Protocol (MCP)
MCP is the standardized language that allows the AI to "touch" the hardware.
      * Server-Client Architecture: The ESP32 runs a lightweight MCP Server (or acts as a proxy to one). It exposes "Tools" and "Resources" to the LLM client.22
      * Tool Definition:
      * get_quantum_fidelity(): A tool that returns the float value of the SCT reconstruction.
      * switch_topology(mode): A tool that actuates the relays to switch between Wye and Delta configurations.
      * pulse_haptic(effect_id): A tool that sends an I2C command to the DRV2605L.
      * Operation: When the user says "Switch to Delta configuration," the DeepSeek model analyzes the intent and outputs a JSON object: { "tool": "switch_topology", "args": { "mode": "delta" } }. The ESP32 parses this JSON and executes the physical switch. This abstraction decouples the AI logic from the low-level register maps.22
6.4 Edge Voice Processing: ESP-Skainet
While DeepSeek provides the "brain," ESP-Skainet provides the "ears." Cloud latency is too high for wake-word detection; the device must listen locally.24
      * WakeNet: The WakeNet9 model runs on the ESP32's NPU instructions. It listens continuously for the phrase "Hi Sorcery." It is highly optimized for the ESP32-S3's instruction set, using minimal CPU cycles.
      * Acoustic Echo Cancellation (AEC): The Audio Front-End (AFE) algorithm uses the dual microphone inputs to filter out the sound coming from the device's own speaker. This allows "barge-in," where the user can interrupt the assistant while it is speaking.
      * MultiNet: For critical commands, the MultiNet7 engine allows offline recognition of up to 200 phrases. If the internet is down, the user can still say "Emergency Stop," and the ESP32 will recognize it locally and actuate the safety relays immediately.24
