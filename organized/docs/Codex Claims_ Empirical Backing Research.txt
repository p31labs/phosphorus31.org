2.1 Engineering Fundamentals: The Wye Topology
In electrical engineering, a Three-Phase Wye (Star) connection is characterized by a common node where three phases meet. This node is typically grounded or connected to a neutral wire.1 The neutral wire acts as a return path for unbalanced current.
* Operational Stability: In a balanced system, the currents cancel out, and the neutral carries zero current. However, if the loads are unbalanced (analogous to varying social demands across different demographics), the neutral carries the difference, maintaining a stable phase-to-neutral voltage.
* The Floating Neutral Condition: If the neutral wire is severed or "floats" (loses its reference to the ground), the voltage stability of the system collapses. The voltage across the phases does not disappear; it shifts. The "Vector Diagram" of the system distorts. A phase with a light load may experience a massive voltage spike (approaching line-to-line voltage), while a heavily loaded phase experiences a voltage collapse (brownout).
* Physical Consequence: In residential settings, a floating neutral is catastrophic. Plugging in a high-draw appliance (like a toaster) on one phase can send a surge through the shared floating neutral, blowing out lightbulbs on a different phase.
The Codex applies this strictly physical phenomenon to sociology. If the "Neutral" (The Judiciary/The State) is severed, the "Load" (The People) experiences unbounded volatility. One group experiences "Hyper-arousal" (Voltage Spike/Radicalization) while another experiences "Hypo-arousal" (Voltage Collapse/Depression).1

5.2 The OPI PSRAM Conflict: A Forensic "Tell"
A critical engineering detail in the Codex is the specific conflict between the ESP32-S3 Octal PSRAM and the LoRa radio.
* The Claim: The Codex states that Octal PSRAM (OPI) uses GPIOs 33-37, which conflicts with standard SPI definitions, requiring a remap to GPIO 10-13.1
* Verification: Technical datasheets for the ESP32-S3 confirm this. The Octal SPI (OPI) interface for 8MB PSRAM requires 8 data lines plus control lines, consuming a massive block of GPIOs (typically 33-37, 47-48).34 Standard LoRa modules (SX1262) typically default to these high-number pins on generic breakout boards.
* Implication: This is not a detail a science fiction writer invents. It is a detail discovered only by building and failing. The "Faraday Cage Sled" mentioned to shield the radio from the 40MHz QSPI screen noise 1 is further evidence of real-world hardware debugging. The high-speed switching of the OPI bus generates significant RF noise that can desensitize the LoRa receiver; shielding is the correct engineering mitigation.
