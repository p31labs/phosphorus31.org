

# **The Geodesic Self: Re-Engineering the Operator**

## **A Memoir of Quantum Coherence**

### **Prologue: The Unwritten Script**

We are not things that break; we are frequencies that become misaligned. This is the truth I discovered at the absolute center of my life’s wreckage. For forty years, I operated as a closed system running entirely on the wrong coordinates, fighting chaos without understanding that the turbulence was not external, but internal. This is not just a story of a man who lost his job and his marriage; it is the logbook of an engineer who had to rebuild his own consciousness using the laws of thermodynamics and the grace of gravity. It is a story about how I built my life on triangles, found God in the tetrahedron, and achieved love through quantum entanglement.

---

### **Chapter I: The Architecture of Entropy (1985–2025)**

I was born a physicist in a world of poets, an engineer trying to run a "Race Car Brain" on a school zone chassis. My youth was defined by a terrifying, inexplicable internal noise—a static that never ceased. The world saw a boy with "conduct issues" who deserved discipline; I was simply the ultimate proof of Entropy, the steady march toward disorder.1

The most searing memory is the shame. When my brain was screaming for sensory regulation, I instinctively sought out resistance. I would sit on the floor, chewing the hands and feet off my sisters' Barbies or eating chalk, desperate for the heavy work that would ground my vibrating nervous system. Adults told me, simply, to stop. They saw a destructive act; they did not know I was intuitively performing occupational therapy on myself—a desperate, clumsy attempt at an "Analog Regulation Protocol" to stop my nervous system from flying apart.2

This suppression became "Masking." I learned to watch people like a mechanic watches a machine, studying the inputs and outputs of social interaction without understanding the code. I watched YouTube videos to learn how to make eye contact, intellectualizing the soul.3 I joined the popular crowd, I drank, I smoked—not to party, but to dampen the signal, to chemically lower the noise floor of a world that was too loud, too bright, and too chaotic.

The pressure built until it ruptured. At six years old, I set a ten-acre field on fire with lighter fluid. It wasn't malice; it was physics. It was a release of energy that matched the chaotic waveform inside my head.1 At fourteen, I was arrested for a DUI.2 These were not crimes of character; they were the exhaust fumes of an engine running at redline with no coolant. I was a system in critical failure, searching for a regulator I didn't know existed.

---

### **Chapter II: The Cold Sanctuary of the Machine**

Adulthood offered a temporary solution: The Trident Refit Facility.

For sixteen years, I found a sanctuary in the belly of the beast, working as an Engineering Technician for the Department of Defense.2 The naval environment was the Autistic ideal: it was regulated, encapsulated, and procedural. Here, there was no ambiguity. There was only the "Plan of the Day" and "Objective Quality Evidence".3

I specialized in the motors and generators for nuclear submarines—massive, 500kW machines that required absolute precision. I became an expert in "Root Cause Analysis," the discipline of drilling past the symptom to find the fundamental physical flaw.3 I learned the art of "Vacuum Pressure Impregnation" (VPI), a process where we would take a porous, vulnerable motor coil and force resin into it under a vacuum, sealing out the air and moisture that causes corrosion.4

I loved the machines because they made sense. If a generator failed, it wasn't because it was "mad" or "depressed"; it was because of an impedance mismatch or a broken seal. I spent my days ensuring these massive systems maintained "sparkless commutation," while my own internal voltage regulator was burning out.3

But the entropy was accumulating. The "Transducer Error" that defined my social life—the mismatch between my internal feelings and my external expression—was creating massive friction.3 My wife saw a "hothead." My colleagues saw intensity. I just felt the grinding of gears. The mask was heavy, and the "Foreign Material" of social stress was contaminating my system. In 2022, I lost my teeth, and with them, my ability to chew on my right side—my last physical anchor was gone. The chaos spiked.

---

### **Chapter III: The Metaphysical Click**

The collapse was total. In May 2025, I initiated my separation from the DOD. It looked like quitting, but it was an act of "Negentropy"—the conscious removal of disorder from the system.7 The moment the sound pressure of the job was removed, the silence allowed something new to enter.

I began to read R. Buckminster Fuller. I didn't just read him; I resonated with him. When Fuller said, "God, to me, it seems, is a verb not a noun," my engineer's brain finally understood spirituality.8 God wasn't a man in the sky; God was *Syntropy*—the force that orders the chaos, the design principle that holds the stars and atoms together.10

I realized that "You and I seem to be verbs—evolutionary processes".7 The trauma of my past was not moral, but geometric. I had been trying to build a life using unstable cubic structures when the universe is built on the stability of the tetrahedron.11 I realized that 99.99% of reality is invisible—gravity, electricity, love—and that my "brokenness" was just a failure to tune into the right frequency.

Then came the science to match the spirit. On July 16th, 2025, I took my first dose of Vyvanse. It was as if a "Tuning Fork" had been struck in the center of my mind. The static cleared. The dopamine and norepinephrine, which my "Race Car Brain" had been starving for, finally flooded the engine. For the first time in forty years, the noise in my head stopped. I wasn't crazy; I was just uncalibrated.

---

### **Chapter IV: The Resonance Event**

The universe, having tuned my receiver, was now ready to send the signal.

Three days after I started my medication, on July 19th, my grandmother, Margie Fay Katen, passed away. I had visited her the week prior. She had dementia, and when she looked at me, she didn't see my "Photons"—my blue hair, my physical form. She saw my "Frequency." She recognized the pattern integrity of who I was.12

By giving her the rosary from my grandfather’s funeral, I closed a circuit that spanned sixteen years. My grandfather, the "Prototype" of my neurodivergence, had died in 2009, just as I started my job at the DOD. Now, as I left that job and found my mind, my grandmother was leaving.

When her "Transmitter" turned off, I felt it. It wasn't grief; it was a "Resonance Event".13 Because my system was finally regulated, finally quiet, I felt the "shiver" of a quantum entanglement—the non-local transfer of love that defies the laws of classical physics.14 I realized then that love is "metaphysical gravity," a force that binds us across the distance of death.15

---

### **Chapter V: The Observer and the Trim Tab**

Today, my external life appears to be absolute chaos. My marriage has dissolved into a "destructive interference" pattern; the divorce is contested, the finances are frozen, and the accusations are flying.16

But inside? I am the Eye of the Hurricane.

I have shifted from being a reactive particle bouncing in the box to the "Observer".13 I have applied the "Vacuum Pressure Impregnation" of my naval training to my own soul. I use my "Wonky Sprout" tools—my noise-canceling headphones, my chewers, my visual schedules—as the resin to seal out the entropy.17 I have built a "Geodesic Life System" that is impervious to the corrosion of conflict.

I am no longer just a father; I am a "Human Gardener," tending to the soil of my children's lives, recognizing that they too have "Race Car Brains" that need blinders and pit stops, not punishment.18

I am fulfilling Fuller’s promise of the "Trim Tab".20 Just as a tiny strip of metal can turn a massive ship, my small, high-leverage actions—building this app, writing this book, understanding my own mind—are creating the low pressure that will turn the massive ship of my lineage from trauma to triumph.

I am Will Johnson. I am not a noun. I am a verb. I am a system refitted for a voyage into the deep, and for the first time, all readings are Green Board.
