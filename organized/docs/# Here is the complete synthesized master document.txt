4.1 Theory: Optimal Transport and Curvature
Ricci curvature, in the discrete context of graphs, is defined via Optimal Transport Theory (Wasserstein Distance). It measures how "fat" or "thin" the geodesic paths are between two nodes.
For an edge $e = (x, y)$ in a graph, the Ollivier-Ricci curvature $\kappa(x, y)$ is defined as 12:
$$\kappa(x, y) = 1 - \frac{W_1(m_x, m_y)}{d(x, y)}$$
where $W_1(m_x, m_y)$ is the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) between the probability measures (neighborhood distributions) of nodes $x$ and $y$, and $d(x, y)$ is the shortest path distance.
